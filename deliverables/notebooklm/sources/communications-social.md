The content mix follows a deliberate ratio: value education at 40% (15 posts), connection and behind-the-scenes content at 30% (11 posts), engagement and polls at 20% (8 posts), and promotion with CTAs at 10% (3 posts). Only 3 of 37 posts ask for anything. The other 34 build the emotional and intellectual foundation that makes those 3 asks feel earned.

**Week 1 (Days 1–7): Pain Awareness.** Surface relatable moments that the audience experiences daily but rarely talks about. 7 posts total. The goal is recognition — every viewer should think "that is me" or "that is my dad."

**Week 4 (Days 22–30): Launch Hype.** Drive waitlist signups with urgency, previews, and countdown content. 13 posts total. The goal is action — every post moves someone from watching to signing up.

## Influencer Outreach Playbook

The influencer strategy operates across 3 tiers with defined budget allocation, target creator counts, and qualification criteria for each level.

### Tier Structure

**Micro Tier (10K–50K followers):** 30 to 50 creators, receiving 40% of the influencer budget. These creators have the highest engagement rates and the most authentic audience relationships. They are the foundation of the program.

**Mid Tier (50K–200K followers):** 10 to 20 creators, receiving 35% of the influencer budget. These creators provide reach while maintaining enough specificity to resonate with the 55–75 demographic and their caregivers.

**Macro Tier (200K+ followers):** 3 to 8 creators, receiving 25% of the influencer budget. These creators provide broad awareness and credibility — particularly important for a new brand entering a space dominated by established hearing aid manufacturers.

### Target Niches

The program targets 5 niches that overlap with the WHSPR Assist audience:

1. **Active Aging** — creators who normalize vibrant, independent life after 55
2. **Caregiver Communities** — creators who speak to the experience of caring for aging parents
3. **Audiology and Optometry Professionals** — licensed providers with educational content platforms
4. **Assistive Tech Reviewers** — creators who evaluate accessibility products with technical rigor
5. **AARP-Adjacent Lifestyle** — creators in the broader boomer lifestyle space covering travel, hobbies, and retirement

### Outreach Messaging

The outreach program uses 15 templates across 3 channels: 4 Instagram DM templates, 4 TikTok DM templates, 4 email templates, and 3 followup step templates.

Six key hooks anchor every outreach message:

- First product combining hearing enhancement and vision assistance in glasses
- Under $350 versus $3,200 for traditional hearing aids
- 80% of adults who need hearing help refuse it — and this solves the #1 reason why
- Looks like regular glasses — nobody knows
- The product their parent will actually wear
- Recommended by audiologists

### Value Proposition for Creators

The value offer is designed to make participation easy and rewarding: a free product valued at $249 to $349, 2 to 4 weeks of early access before public availability, a 10% affiliate commission on all sales driven, exclusive content and behind-the-scenes access, complete creative freedom with no scripts and no talking points, a caregiver bonus consisting of a second unit for a parent or family member, and cross-promotion through WHSPR Assist's own channels.

### Followup Sequence

The followup cadence is deliberate and restrained. Day 0 is the initial outreach. Day 3 is a warm bump that adds new value — a data point, a press mention, a backer milestone — rather than simply asking again. Day 7 is a final touch that leaves the door open without pressure. Three total touches maximum per creator. The brand's dignity-first positioning extends to how it treats creators — no spam, no desperation, no "just checking in."

### Qualification Criteria

Creators must meet specific thresholds before outreach begins. Audience demographics must show 60% or more in the target age range and interests. Engagement rate minimums are tiered: above 3% for micro creators, above 2% for mid-tier, and above 1.5% for macro. Brand safety screening eliminates any creator who mocks aging, uses ageist humor, or positions assistive technology as something to be embarrassed about. Dignity-first alignment is non-negotiable.

### Tracking and Attribution

Every creator receives a unique discount code formatted as WHSPR-[CREATORNAME] and a unique referral link formatted as whspr.com/r/[creatorname]. This dual-tracking system provides clear attribution for both immediate conversions and delayed purchases that come through organic content discovery.

## Review Response Playbook

The review response strategy covers every scenario from enthusiastic 5-star reviews to coordinated troll campaigns. The voice is Dr. Maren — warm, direct, ownership-oriented, and never defensive. Every response reinforces the brand's values whether the review is glowing or hostile.

### 5-Star Review Responses

The playbook includes 5 templates for positive reviews. The strategy is consistent: thank the reviewer by name, reflect the specific benefit they mentioned back to them, express genuine warmth, and close with a soft CTA to share their experience. The tone is calibrated to warmth 9 out of 10 and formality 3 out of 10. Responses run 3 to 5 sentences.

The goal is not just gratitude — it is amplification. When a reviewer says "I heard my granddaughter's joke for the first time," the response reflects that specific moment back, validating their experience and creating a story that other readers can see themselves in.

### 1-Star Review Responses

The playbook includes 8 templates covering the most common complaint categories. The strategy follows a consistent pattern: acknowledge the frustration without qualifying it, own the problem without making excuses, and move the conversation offline to care@whspr.com. The tone is calibrated to warmth 8 out of 10 and formality 4 out of 10. Responses run 4 to 6 sentences.

The complaint categories covered are: battery life, hearing clarity, comfort, AI features, price value, setup difficulty, customer service, and shipping.

Known product limitations are addressed directly rather than deflected. Battery life is 4 to 5 hours — the response acknowledges this as a real constraint and explains the engineering tradeoff. The product is not a hearing aid replacement for severe loss — the response is transparent about this boundary. New brand trust concerns are met with specifics about the team, the technology, and the support infrastructure, not with vague reassurances.

### Troll Review Responses

The playbook includes 2 templates for illegitimate reviews. The strategy is brief and dignified: a short response that addresses any factual claims, a report to the platform when appropriate, and no further engagement. The tone is calibrated to warmth 5 out of 10 and formality 6 out of 10. Responses are limited to 2 to 3 sentences.

Troll identification signals include: no verified purchase, generic language that could apply to any product, and promotion of a competitor product within the review. The response never accuses the reviewer of being a troll — it simply addresses the content at face value, provides accurate information, and moves on.

### Escalation Framework

Review responses operate across 3 escalation levels:

**Level 1 — Standard (90% of reviews):** Handled within 24 to 48 hours using the template library. These are routine positive, negative, and neutral reviews that fall within normal product feedback.

**Level 2 — Product Safety (8% of reviews):** Escalated within 12 hours to the product team. These involve any mention of physical discomfort, electrical issues, or unexpected product behavior that could indicate a safety concern.

**Level 3 — Legal or Coordinated (2% of reviews):** Escalated immediately to the communications lead. These involve potential legal claims, coordinated review campaigns from competitors, or reviews that reference regulatory or compliance issues.

### Language Rules

The review response playbook enforces the same word rules as the broader brand voice. The team never uses: hearing loss solution, disability, elderly, senior, "we apologize for any inconvenience," or "per our policy." The team always uses: hear more clearly, see more sharply, smart glasses, hearing enhancement, and straightforward. These word rules apply to every response, whether the review is a glowing 5-star testimonial or an angry 1-star complaint. The language is the brand.
