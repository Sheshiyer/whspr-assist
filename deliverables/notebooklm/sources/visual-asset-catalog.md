# WHSPR Assist — Visual System Reference

## The Visual System at a Glance

The WHSPR Assist visual system is a library of fifty-four generated images that collectively define how the brand appears across every channel — from app store listings and Instagram Stories to investor pitch decks, audiologist clinic displays, and campaign landing pages. Every image was generated through a controlled AI pipeline using three distinct models, two primary seed values for variant comparison, and a comprehensive negative prompt that enforces brand integrity at the point of creation rather than after the fact. The result is a cohesive visual language that feels warm, clinically credible, and unmistakably human — never cold, never patronizing, never science-fiction.

Every file follows the naming convention `[ID]-[name]-[model]-v[seed].png`, where `v1` maps to seed forty-two, `v137` to seed one hundred thirty-seven, and `v256` to seed two hundred fifty-six. This makes every asset traceable to its generation parameters.

---

## Generation Models and Their Roles

Three AI image generation models were employed across the library, each selected for specific strengths that map to particular asset categories.

### Flux 2 Pro

Flux 2 Pro was the model of choice for precision brand assets that demand clean typography, geometric accuracy, and crisp detail. Its strength lies in rendering hard-edged materials — brushed titanium, embossed linen, glass surfaces — with photographic fidelity. Across the WHSPR Assist library, Flux 2 Pro generated the brand seal, logo emboss treatments, capsule collection product shots, hero book photography, essence vial compositions, product flatlay arrangements, all three icon system sets, and the companion app icons. When the visual system requires a surface that catches light with metallic precision or an icon grid that holds at sixteen pixels, Flux 2 Pro is the engine behind it.

### NanoBananaPro

NanoBananaPro was deployed for lifestyle photography, narrative sequences, campaign imagery, and environmental shots — any composition requiring warm natural light, authentic human subjects, and contextual storytelling. This model excels at rendering human faces and bodies in natural environments with the kind of soft directional light that the brand photography guidelines demand. NanoBananaPro generated the brand kit bento grids, catalog layouts, campaign grids, contact sheet narratives, all seeker persona posters, both campaign posters, both nine-panel narrative sequences, and every digital asset including app screenshots, email heroes, Instagram Story templates, and pitch deck hero images. When the visual system needs a sixty-eight-year-old man in a garden doorway holding a coffee cup with morning light on his face, NanoBananaPro is the model that delivers.

### Recraft

Recraft served a specialized role, generating illustration-specific outputs that require a heritage engraving aesthetic or an art panel visual language. Its rendering style produces clean line work with a warmth and tactility that neither Flux 2 Pro nor NanoBananaPro can replicate in illustration mode. Recraft generated the heritage engraving illustrations, the art panel compositions, and one alternative variant of the vision features icon set. These assets exist in both PNG and WebP formats, reflecting the model's output pipeline. Recraft's contribution is smaller in volume — seven files including format variants — but essential to the brand's illustration identity.

## Seed Strategy and Variant Logic

Two primary seeds ran across all fifty-four assets. Seed forty-two (v1) is the primary variant for selection — the "first pick" that the creative team evaluates first. ## Photography Guidelines

The photography direction for WHSPR Assist is defined by warmth, dignity, and authentic human context. Every photograph targets a Canon R5 aesthetic at fifty millimeters, f/2.8, warm natural color grade. Light arrives from the left or behind-left, evoking golden hour warmth at fifty-two hundred to fifty-eight hundred Kelvin with five to ten points of added warmth in post. The color grade carries slightly lifted shadows and true-to-life skin tones with warmth bias. The visual reference sits between Warby Parker campaigns and AARP editorial photography.

### Photography Scenarios

Eight approved scenarios define the brand's photographic vocabulary. A family dinner scene shows the subject hearing a granddaughter clearly by candlelight. A morning routine captures picking up glasses from a maple charging cradle. A coffee-with-friend scene places two adults in their late sixties in conversation. A clinic moment positions an audiologist and patient at eye level. A garden reading scene, a golf course scene with the Active frame, a grocery independence scene with AI vision reading a label, and a prescription handoff across a warm wooden desk complete the set.

### The Absolute Constraints

The photography prohibitions protect the brand's emotional contract. Never show an elderly person struggling. Never position subjects as confused or dependent. No isolation imagery. No cold or blue lighting. The product must never appear without human context. Minimum age for primary subjects is fifty. The rule: always show dignity and independence — no infantilizing poses, no "old person struggling" tropes.

## Illustration Guidelines

The illustration system follows a clean medical-grade line aesthetic with warm teal accents — Apple Health meets Phonak marketing clarity. Lines are two pixels in weight with round caps, round joins, and two-pixel corner radius. Primary line color is Trust Navy or Charcoal Ink; fills use Clarity Teal at twenty to forty percent opacity. Maximum three colors per illustration; no gradients. Subject matter: how-it-works diagrams, feature callouts, clinical workflow diagrams, and instructional sequences.

Prohibited: cartoon faces, mascots, decorative patterns, isometric or 3D rendering, anime or pixel art, and disability depictions.

## Icon System Specifications

The icon system is built on a twenty-four-by-twenty-four-pixel grid with a two-pixel safe zone. Icons scale across five sizes: sixteen, twenty-four, thirty-two, forty-eight, and sixty-four pixels. Stroke weight is one-and-a-half pixels at the base size, scaling proportionally. Style is outline only, single color default. Colors: Trust Navy on light backgrounds, Warm Linen on dark, Clarity Teal for active states. Minimum touch target: forty-four by forty-four pixels.

## Negative Prompt — Universal Exclusions

Every image was generated with a comprehensive negative prompt that excludes visual territories incompatible with the brand. The excluded territories include: patronizing elderly imagery; walkers or wheelchairs unless contextually relevant; isolation or sadness; sterile white clinical environments; cold fluorescent lighting; generic stock elderly photography; tech-bro aesthetic; young models wearing the product; gaming or creator contexts; anime, cartoon, pixel art, or low-poly styles; vaporwave, glitch art, cyberpunk, or synthwave aesthetics; hearing aid stigma imagery; disability-focused framing; excessive chrome; neon lighting; products floating in white space with specification callouts; before-and-after deficit comparisons; extreme ear close-ups; Dutch angles; forced perspective; heavy gradients; lens flares; and HDR over-processing.

This negative prompt codifies the principle that WHSPR Assist shows people living well, not struggling to live. It enforces warmth over sterility, dignity over pity, and natural context over artificial isolation.
