# Video Scripts

> *Hear more. See more. Live more.*

**Format:** Crowdfunding campaign video (Kickstarter / Indiegogo)
**Duration:** 150 seconds (2:30)
**Voice:** Compassionate Companion (Dr. Maren register)
**Reading Level:** Grade 6–7
**Voiceover Words:** 382 (2.55 words/second — warm, measured pace)

---

## Emotional Arc

```
Hook → Problem + Hero → Product Reveal → How It Works →
Feature Highlights → Social Proof → Offer → CTA
```

### Music Mood Arc

Solo acoustic guitar → piano enters → strings build → full arrangement → strips back for intimacy → rebuilds → resolves to solo piano

---

## Section 1: Hook (0:00–0:10)

**Emotional Beat:** Recognition — "That is me. That is my father."

**Visual:** Tight close-up on George's face. Golden evening light. Out-of-focus family laughing around him. Audio is muffled — we hear what George hears. Voices indistinct. He smiles. A beat too late.

**Voiceover:**
> "He heard the laughter. He just could not catch the joke."

**Music:** Solo fingerpicked acoustic guitar, muffled dinner ambience.

---

## Section 2: Problem + Hero (0:10–0:30)

**Emotional Beat:** Empathy and identification

**Visual Montage:**
1. George at a restaurant, leaning forward, trying to hear the waiter
2. George holding a prescription bottle at arm's length, squinting
3. George's TV remote showing volume at 38/50, wife covering one ear
4. Audiologist's office — George looks at hearing aids on the table, not hostile, just... not ready
5. George walks out into daylight, pausing at the door

**Voiceover:**
> "George heard the audiologist. He heard the price — thirty-two hundred dollars. He looked at the hearing aids on the table, and he said he would think about it. That was two years ago.
>
> He is not stubborn. He just wants to hear more clearly without the whole world knowing he needs help."

**On-Screen Text:**
- 0:12 → `George Mitchell | Age 68 | Retired school administrator`
- 0:22 → `Average cost of hearing aids: $3,200 per pair`

---

## Section 3: Product Reveal (0:30–0:45)

**Emotional Beat:** Curiosity and surprise — "Wait... those are just glasses?"

**Visual:** Close-up of WHSPR Assist Rx on Dr. Priya's desk beside an audiogram. She picks them up. Hands them to George. He puts them on. Mirror shot — he looks like himself. Just wearing good glasses. The faintest smile.

**Voiceover:**
> "Dr. Priya reached into her desk. Not for hearing aids. For these.
>
> WHSPR Assist Rx. Prescription-ready smart glasses with hearing enhancement, an AI-powered camera, and a frame that looks exactly like the glasses George already wears."

**On-Screen Text:**
- 0:32 → `WHSPR Assist Rx`
- 0:40 → `Prescription glasses. Hearing enhancement. AI vision.`

**Music:** Piano lead with warm strings entering — the emotional lift.

---

## Section 4: How It Works (0:45–1:15)

**Emotional Beat:** Clarity and confidence — straightforward process

**Visual Sequence:**
1. **Step 1:** George at clinic, Dr. Priya uploads audiogram on tablet
2. **Step 2:** George at home, morning routine, glasses on bedside charging stand
3. **Step 3:** Breakfast table, George hears Margaret clearly for the first time in years

**Voiceover:**
> "Step one: your audiologist uploads your hearing profile. Takes five minutes.
>
> Step two: the glasses calibrate to your audiogram. The microphones learn what you need to hear and what is just noise.
>
> Step three: you put them on. You hear the conversation. You hear the person, not the room. That is it."

**On-Screen Text:**
- 0:47 → `Step 1: Hearing profile uploaded`
- 0:55 → `Step 2: Calibrated to your audiogram`
- 1:03 → `Step 3: Hear more clearly`
- 1:10 → `No fitting appointments. No adjustment period.`

---

## Section 5: Feature Highlights (1:15–1:45)

**Emotional Beat:** Impressed and informed

**Visual Sequence:**
1. Split audio demo — left ear: restaurant noise; right ear: WHSPR-processed clarity
2. George holds prescription bottle, taps frame, AI voice reads label aloud
3. Frame lineup — all four models on frosted glass surface
4. Optometrist fitting prescription lenses into WHSPR Assist frame

**Voiceover:**
> "Dual microphones with active noise cancellation separate the voice in front of you from everything else.
>
> A 32-megapixel AI camera reads prescription labels, menus, and street signs — aloud, through your glasses.
>
> Four frame styles. Your prescription lenses fit inside. Bluetooth 5.4 for calls and audio. Magnetic charging overnight.
>
> Hearing enhancement, vision assistance, and AI — in one pair of glasses."

---

## Section 6: Social Proof (1:45–2:00)

**Emotional Beat:** Trust and validation

**Three Talking Heads:**

**George Mitchell (End-user, 68):**
> "Lily told me about her science project yesterday. The whole thing. I heard every word. And she did not have to repeat herself."

**Dr. Priya Sharma (Audiologist):**
> "Forty percent of my patients decline hearing aids. Now I have something to offer them instead of nothing."

**Sarah Mitchell (Caregiver daughter):**
> "I stopped worrying about him at dinner. He is back in the conversation. That is all I wanted."

**Music:** Strip back to piano + guitar, intimate warmth.

---

## Section 7: Offer (2:00–2:15)

**Emotional Beat:** Value clarity — the price comparison lands

**Visual:** Price comparison graphic, product flat lay, tier pricing display.

**Voiceover:**
> "A pair of hearing aids costs thirty-two hundred dollars or more. WHSPR Assist Rx starts at two hundred forty-nine dollars. Super Early Bird for the first two hundred backers. Early Bird at two seventy-nine. Standard at two ninety-nine.
>
> Buying for a parent? The Caregiver Bundle is two pairs for four ninety-nine."

---

## Section 8: Call to Action (2:15–2:30)

**Emotional Beat:** Warm resolution and motivated action

**Visual:** George and Lily at the dinner table. This time, he leans in. She talks fast. He follows every word. He laughs — right on time. Logo. Tagline. Campaign URL.

**Voiceover:**
> "This is not about hearing aids. This is about hearing your granddaughter's story. Reading the menu without asking. Being part of the conversation again.
>
> Hear more. See more. Live more.
>
> Back WHSPR Assist Rx today."

**Music:** Full arrangement resolving to solo piano, sustained final chord.
