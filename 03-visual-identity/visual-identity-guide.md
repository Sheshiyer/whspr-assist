## 1. Visual Strategy

### Mood Keywords

gentle-clarity, warm-clinical, dignified-aging, accessible-premium, trusted-technology, compassionate-design, clean-confidence, soft-authority, family-warmth, independence

### Visual Mission

Establish a visual system that communicates clinical credibility and human warmth in equal measure. Every visual element must pass the **Dignity Test**: would George Mitchell feel respected, capable, and empowered looking at this? The visual identity bridges the gap between sterile medical-device aesthetics and patronizing consumer marketing for older adults. WHSPR Assist looks like what it is: premium eyewear recommended by a healthcare professional who genuinely cares about the person wearing it.

### Design Philosophy

The WHSPR Assist visual system is built on three foundational principles:

1. **Clinical Warmth** -- Professional credibility delivered through warm, human-centered presentation. The visual equivalent of a well-lit audiologist's office where the technology feels like a trusted friend, not a medical device.

2. **Dignity-First Design** -- Every visual choice communicates capability, not compensation. Enhancement framing in imagery, language, and layout. Deficit framing is never permitted.

3. **Accessible Premium** -- The system must feel premium enough to justify clinical recommendation while remaining approachable enough that George does not feel he is being sold to. Think Warby Parker's warmth meets Apple Health's clarity meets Phonak's clinical authority.

### Visual Territory

WHSPR Assist occupies a specific visual territory that no competitor currently holds:

- **Not sterile clinical** (hospital equipment, cold fluorescent lighting, white-on-white)
- **Not tech-bro minimalist** (dark mode, neon accents, geometric brutalism)
- **Not patronizing senior** (stock-photo smiles, oversized buttons, primary colors)
- **Not budget OTC** (plastic packaging, discount badges, generic amplifier aesthetic)

Instead, WHSPR Assist occupies the **warm clinical** space: clean layouts with natural light warmth, professional typography with human-scaled spacing, and a color palette that communicates both trust and comfort.

## 3. Typography

### Type System Overview

The WHSPR Assist type system uses three font families, each serving a distinct communication role. All three are open-source Google Fonts, ensuring zero licensing cost and universal availability across web, print, and application contexts.

### Header Font: Source Serif 4

**Purpose:** Headlines, section titles, hero text, pull quotes, product names  
**Source:** [Google Fonts -- Source Serif 4](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Serif+4)  
**Classification:** Transitional serif  
**Weights Used:** Regular (400), SemiBold (600)  
**License:** SIL Open Font License 1.1

| Context | Weight | Size Range | Line Height | Letter Spacing | Case |
|---------|--------|-----------|-------------|----------------|------|
| Hero headline (H1) | SemiBold 600 | 40-56px / 2.5-3.5rem | 1.15 | -0.02em | Sentence case |
| Section headline (H2) | SemiBold 600 | 32-40px / 2.0-2.5rem | 1.2 | -0.01em | Sentence case |
| Subsection (H3) | Regular 400 | 24-32px / 1.5-2.0rem | 1.25 | 0em | Sentence case |
| Card title (H4) | SemiBold 600 | 20-24px / 1.25-1.5rem | 1.3 | 0em | Sentence case |
| Pull quote | Regular 400 | 24-28px / 1.5-1.75rem | 1.4 | 0em | Sentence case |
| Product name | SemiBold 600 | 18-22px / 1.125-1.375rem | 1.3 | 0.02em | Title Case (exception) |

**Rationale:** Source Serif 4 carries the authority of a serif typeface without the formality of a traditional didone or the stuffiness of Times New Roman. Its transitional letterforms feel contemporary yet established -- the typographic equivalent of Dr. Maren's calm authority. SemiBold weight for display creates visual hierarchy without shouting. Regular weight for emphasis creates distinction without heaviness.

**Critical Rule: Sentence case always.** The only exception is proper product names (WHSPR Assist Rx, Assist Rx Classic). Never use ALL CAPS for headlines -- it reads as shouting and violates the dignity-first principle. Title Case is reserved exclusively for product names.

## 4. Logo Usage

### The WHSPR Assist Sigil

The WHSPR Assist sigil (logo mark) is a unified symbol representing hearing and vision in harmony. It consists of three components:

1. **Sound Wave Arc** -- A flowing arc emanating from the left side, representing hearing enhancement. The arc curves protectively around the lens element, suggesting care and envelopment. Two to three concentric arcs of decreasing thickness create a sense of gentle sound radiation.

2. **Lens Circle** -- A perfect circle on the right side of the composition, representing vision correction and the physical glasses lens. The circle is rendered with a subtle inner highlight to suggest optical clarity.

3. **Connection Dot** -- A small filled circle at the intersection point where the sound wave arc meets the lens circle. This dot represents the digital ecosystem -- the companion app, the Provider Portal, the clinical connection between patient and professional.

### Logo Construction

**Grid:** The sigil is constructed on a 48x48 unit grid. The lens circle occupies the right third (units 32-48 horizontal, 12-36 vertical). The sound wave arcs originate from the left edge (unit 0) at vertical center (unit 24) and sweep rightward, their outer arc reaching the lens circle perimeter. The connection dot is centered at approximately unit 30, 18 (intersection of arc and lens).

**Proportions:** The total width-to-height ratio of the sigil is approximately 1.2:1. The lens circle diameter is 50% of the total height. The sound wave arcs span 75% of the total width.

**Stroke Weights:**
- Outer sound wave arc: 2.5 units
- Inner sound wave arc: 1.5 units
- Lens circle outline: 2.0 units
- Connection dot: 4.0 unit diameter (filled)

### Logo Versions

**Primary Logo (Horizontal):** Sigil + "WHSPR Assist" wordmark to the right, set in Source Serif 4 SemiBold. The wordmark baseline aligns with the bottom of the lens circle. Spacing between sigil and wordmark: 1x the lens circle diameter.

**Secondary Logo (Stacked):** Sigil centered above "WHSPR Assist" wordmark. Vertical spacing between sigil bottom and wordmark top: 0.75x the lens circle diameter.

**Sigil Only (Icon):** Used when the brand name is already established in context -- app icons, favicon, social media avatars, repeat brand touchpoints. Minimum 32x32px for digital, 10mm for print.

**Wordmark Only:** "WHSPR Assist" in Source Serif 4 SemiBold. Used in text-dense layouts where the sigil would be too small to render clearly. Minimum 80px wide for digital, 25mm for print.

### Color Treatments

| Version | Background | Sigil Color | Wordmark Color | Usage |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Primary | Warm Linen or White | Trust Navy | Trust Navy | Default for light backgrounds |
| Reversed | Trust Navy or Deep Navy | Warm Linen | Warm Linen | Dark backgrounds, hero sections |
| Teal Accent | White or Warm Linen | Clarity Teal (arcs) + Trust Navy (lens) | Trust Navy | Feature highlights, hearing content |
| Monochrome Light | Any light background | Trust Navy | Trust Navy | Single-color printing |
| Monochrome Dark | Any dark background | White (#FFFFFF) | White (#FFFFFF) | Single-color reverse printing |
| Grayscale | Any | 60% black (arcs), 80% black (lens) | 80% black | Grayscale printing, fax |

### Clear Space

**Minimum clear space** around the logo equals **1x the height of the lens circle** on all sides. No text, imagery, borders, or other graphic elements may enter this space.

```
     [1x]
      |
[1x]--[SIGIL + WORDMARK]--[1x]
      |
     [1x]
```

For the stacked version, clear space is measured from the widest element (either sigil or wordmark) and extends 1x lens circle height from the outer boundary.

### Minimum Size

| Format | Horizontal Logo | Stacked Logo | Sigil Only | Wordmark Only |
|--------|----------------|--------------|------------|---------------|
| Digital (px) | 160px wide | 80px wide | 32px | 80px wide |
| Print (mm) | 50mm wide | 25mm wide | 10mm | 25mm wide |
| Embossed/Debossed | 60mm wide | 30mm wide | 12mm | 30mm wide |

Below minimum sizes, the sigil details (connection dot, inner arc) become illegible. Use the wordmark only version at very small sizes.

### Logo Misuse (Prohibited Treatments)

- **Never** rotate the logo at any angle
- **Never** stretch, distort, or alter proportions
- **Never** apply gradients, shadows, or 3D effects to the logo
- **Never** place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid or frosted panel
- **Never** change the typeface of the wordmark
- **Never** rearrange the components (arcs, lens, dot)
- **Never** use colors outside the approved palette
- **Never** add outlines, strokes, or borders to the logo
- **Never** place the logo inside a shape container (circle, square, etc.)
- **Never** animate the sigil components independently
- **Never** crop any portion of the logo or sigil
- **Never** reduce below minimum size

### Logo on Packaging and Product

**On-Product (Glasses Frame):** The wordmark "WHSPR" is debossed on the inner left temple arm. The sigil is not used on-product due to size constraints. Font: Source Serif 4 SemiBold, debossed into warm linen-colored temple material.

**Packaging (Box Lid):** Primary horizontal logo centered on lid, Trust Navy on Warm Linen stock. Minimum 50mm wide. Debossed with subtle texture difference.

**Packaging (Care Kit):** Sigil-only debossed on warm maple charging cradle surface. Minimum 12mm.

**Clinical Materials:** Primary horizontal logo in header position, left-aligned, with 1x clear space from page edges. Trust Navy on white stock for clinical brochures.

## 5. Imagery & Art Direction

### Photography Style

**Overarching Philosophy:** Every photograph must communicate independence, capability, and warmth. The subject is always an engaged, competent adult living their life with enhanced clarity -- not a patient receiving care. The product is a supporting character, never the hero person in the shot.

**Light Direction:**
- Primary: Warm natural light from the left or behind-left (golden hour warmth)
- Secondary: Soft diffused daylight (overcast day quality)
- Never: Direct flash, cold fluorescent, overhead institutional lighting
- Color temperature: 5200-5800K with warm grade (+5 to +10 warmth in post)

**Camera and Lens:**
- Reference camera: Canon R5
- Primary lens: 50mm f/2.8 (natural perspective, gentle background separation)
- Environmental: 35mm f/2.8 (wider context shots, clinic interiors, family gatherings)
- Detail: 85mm f/2.0 (product close-ups, temple gesture details, face portraits)
- Never: Wide-angle distortion (24mm or wider), extreme telephoto compression, fisheye

**Color Grade:**
- Warm natural palette with slightly lifted shadows
- Skin tones: True-to-life with warmth bias, never desaturated or overly graded
- Highlights: Slightly warm, not blown
- Shadows: Lifted 5-10%, revealing detail without flat appearance
- Reference: Warby Parker campaign photography meets AARP editorial

**Depth of Field:**
- Subject sharp, background with gentle bokeh (f/2.8 to f/4)
- Product shots: f/5.6 to f/8 for full-product sharpness
- Environmental: f/4 for context with focus on subject
- Never: f/1.4 extreme bokeh (too stylized), f/11+ deep focus (too clinical/documentary)

### Photography Subjects

**Primary Subjects (75% of imagery):**
- Adults aged 50-75, diverse in ethnicity, body type, and personal style
- Must appear naturally groomed, appropriately dressed for context
- Expressions: Engaged, attentive, content, focused -- never mugging, never staged-smiling
- Posture: Upright, confident, naturally comfortable

**Secondary Subjects (15% of imagery):**
- Multi-generational family groupings (grandparent + child/grandchild)
- Clinical professionals (audiologist, optometrist) in professional settings
- Couples aged 55+ in shared activities

**Environmental/Detail (10% of imagery):**
- Product on surfaces (frosted glass, warm maple, cotton linen)
- Charging cradle in morning light
- Companion app on iPhone screen
- Clinic interior details (frame display, audiogram screen)

### Photography Scenarios: DO

1. **Family Dinner** -- George seated at a warmly lit dinner table, leaning in to hear his granddaughter across the table. Natural candlelight. He is smiling at something she said. Glasses visible but not the focus.

2. **Morning Routine** -- A woman in her early 60s picking up her WHSPR Assist glasses from the maple charging cradle beside her bed. Morning light through sheer curtains. She is beginning her day with confidence.

3. **Coffee with a Friend** -- Two men in their late 60s at a cafe, deep in conversation. One wears WHSPR Assist glasses. Warm ambient light. Both are engaged and animated. Background slightly soft.

4. **Clinic Moment** -- An audiologist (40s) showing frame options to a patient (60s) in a warm, well-lit office. Both seated at eye level. Patient is trying on a frame, looking in a mirror, and appearing pleased. Professional but not intimidating.

5. **Garden Reading** -- A man sitting in a garden chair reading a newspaper on a bright morning. Glasses catching warm light. Green foliage soft in background. Peaceful, independent, unhurried.

6. **Golf Course** -- Active sport frame (V06-T) on a man lining up a putt. Outdoor natural light. Athletic posture, concentration, social context with buddies visible in soft background.

7. **Grocery Independence** -- A woman using AI vision assist to read a product label in a well-lit grocery store. Subtle teal UI overlay suggests the AI reading function without heavy tech emphasis.

8. **Prescription Handoff** -- An optometrist handing a branded case to a patient across a warm wooden desk. Both are smiling. The case looks premium. The moment feels like receiving something special, not medical.

### Photography Scenarios: DO NOT

1. **Never** show an elderly person struggling to hear, cupping their ear, or looking confused
2. **Never** show isolation, loneliness, or sadness as the "before" state
3. **Never** use walkers, wheelchairs, or mobility aids unless the scenario specifically requires it (and even then, the subject must be shown as capable and in control)
4. **Never** show a younger person "helping" an older person use the product (implies incapability)
5. **Never** use stock photography with the generic "silver-haired couple walking on beach" aesthetic
6. **Never** show the product floating in white space with tech-spec callouts (tech-bro aesthetic)
7. **Never** show models under 50 wearing the product in primary marketing (secondary or family context only)
8. **Never** photograph in cold fluorescent lighting or sterile white environments
9. **Never** show the product next to traditional hearing aids (invites comparison to what we are not)
10. **Never** use forced perspective, dutch angles, or dramatic shadows (too editorial/tech)
11. **Never** show exaggerated "delighted" reactions (patronizing and inauthentic)
12. **Never** photograph subjects from above looking down (infantilizing camera angle)
13. **Never** show gaming, VR, content creation, or social media use cases (wrong audience)
14. **Never** show the product in neon, vaporwave, or cyberpunk lighting contexts

### Illustration Style

**Philosophy:** Illustrations serve an informational purpose -- they explain, guide, and clarify. They are never decorative filler. Every illustration must communicate a specific piece of information that would be harder to convey with photography alone.

**Visual References:**
- Apple Health app illustration aesthetic (clean, warm, accessible)
- Phonak marketing clarity (informational precision)
- Warby Parker's friendly technical drawings

**Line Style:**
- Clean, consistent line weight: 2px at standard view (1024px wide)
- Rounded line caps and joins (corner radius: 2px)
- No hand-drawn wobble or sketch aesthetic
- Single-color line work with optional Clarity Teal fill accents

**Color Usage in Illustration:**
- Primary line color: Trust Navy (#1B365D) or Charcoal Ink (#2C3E50)
- Accent fills: Clarity Teal (#2A9D8F) at 20-40% opacity for feature highlights
- Background: White or Warm Linen
- Never use gradients in illustrations
- Never use more than 3 colors per illustration (line color + 1 accent + background)

**Subject Matter:**
- How-it-works diagrams (hearing enhancement visualization, AI text recognition flow)
- Product feature callouts (temple gesture controls, charging mechanism, lens options)
- Clinical workflow diagrams (audiogram calibration, Provider Portal, fitting process)
- Step-by-step instructional sequences (setup, daily use, charging, app connection)

**Illustration DON'Ts:**
- No cartoon faces or character illustrations
- No mascots or anthropomorphized objects

## 6. Graphic Elements

### Iconography System

**Grid and Construction:**
All icons are built on a 24x24 pixel grid with a 2px safe zone (active drawing area: 20x20px). This grid scales proportionally to 16px, 32px, 48px, and 64px variants.

**Line Properties:**
- Stroke weight: 1.5px at 24px size (scales proportionally)
- Line cap: Round
- Line join: Round
- Corner radius: 2px (for rectangular elements within icons)
- Optical alignment: Icons are optically centered within the bounding box, not mathematically centered

**Style:**
- Outline-only at default state (no fills)
- Single color: Trust Navy on light backgrounds, Warm Linen on dark backgrounds
- Active/selected state: Clarity Teal with optional 15% fill
- Disabled state: Soft Silver (#B0BEC5)

**Minimum Sizes:**
| Size | Usage | Touch Target |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 16px | Inline text icons, badges | N/A (not interactive) |
| 24px | Standard UI icons, navigation | 44x44px touch target |
| 32px | Feature cards, section markers | 48x48px touch target |
| 48px | Hero feature icons, marketing | N/A |
| 64px | Large display, presentations | N/A |

**Critical Accessibility Rule:** All interactive icons must maintain a 44x44px minimum touch target regardless of visual icon size. This is non-negotiable for the 55-75 audience who may have reduced fine motor precision.

### Spacing System

The WHSPR Assist spacing system uses a 4px base unit with an 8px standard increment:

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| space-xs | 4px | Inline icon gaps, tight padding |
| space-sm | 8px | Between related elements, icon-to-label |
| space-md | 16px | Between content blocks, card padding (mobile) |
| space-lg | 24px | Section padding, card padding (desktop) |
| space-xl | 32px | Between major sections |
| space-2xl | 48px | Major section separators, hero padding |
| space-3xl | 64px | Page-level vertical rhythm |
| space-4xl | 96px | Hero section vertical padding |

**Critical Rule:** Touch targets for interactive elements require minimum 44px height/width with at least 8px spacing between adjacent targets. For the 55-75 audience, generous spacing is a functional requirement, not a stylistic choice.

### Grid System

**Desktop (1200px+):** 12-column grid, 1120px max-width container, 24px gutters, 48px outer margins
**Tablet (768-1199px):** 8-column grid, 720px max-width container, 20px gutters, 24px outer margins
**Mobile (320-767px):** 4-column grid, full-width container, 16px gutters, 16px outer margins

Content blocks span:
- Full-width hero: 12 columns / 8 columns / 4 columns
- Text content: 8 columns max (for optimal 65-75 character line length at 20px body text)
- Sidebar: 4 columns (desktop only)
- Cards: 4 columns desktop / 4 columns tablet / 4 columns mobile (stack vertically)

## Appendix A: Negative Prompt (Visual Do-Not-Generate)

The following visual directions are prohibited across all WHSPR Assist brand materials, including photography, illustration, UI design, packaging, AI-generated imagery, and social media content:

**People and Pose:**
- Patronizing elderly imagery (condescending smiles, thumbs-up, exaggerated delight)
- Walker or wheelchair imagery unless the scenario specifically requires it
- Isolation, loneliness, or sadness as emotional framing
- Infantilizing poses (being helped by younger people, confusion, dependency)
- Models under age 50 wearing the product in primary marketing
- Generic stock-photo elderly (silver-haired couple on beach, grandmother baking cookies)
- Hearing-aid stigma imagery (cupping ear, struggling to hear, embarrassment)
- Disability-focused framing (before/after showing deficit, "suffering from" visual equivalent)

**Aesthetic and Style:**
- Sterile white clinical (hospital equipment, fluorescent lighting, white-on-white)
- Cold fluorescent lighting or blue-tinted environments
- Tech-bro minimalism (dark mode, neon accents, geometric brutalism, glass morphism)
- Budget OTC amplifier plastic aesthetic (cheap packaging, discount badges)
- Chrome excess, reflective surfaces, metallic futurism
- Neon colors, cyberpunk, synthwave, vaporwave aesthetics
- Glitch art, pixel art, low poly, retro gaming
- Anime, cartoon, or illustrated character style
- Heavy gradients, lens flares, or HDR over-processing
- Young creator/influencer contexts (gaming, streaming, social media)

**Compositional:**
- Product floating in white space with tech-spec callout lines
- Split-screen before/after comparisons
- Extreme close-ups of ear canals or hearing mechanisms
- Dutch angles, forced perspective, dramatic shadows
- Busy or cluttered compositions with more than 3 focal elements
- Product comparison layouts with competing hearing aids
