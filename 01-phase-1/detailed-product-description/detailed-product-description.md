# Detailed Product Description: WHSPR Assist Rx

## Overview

WHSPR Assist Rx is the first assistive smart glasses system that combines hearing enhancement, camera-based vision assistance, and multimodal AI in a single prescription-ready frame -- all at a fraction of the cost of traditional hearing aids or vision-assistive devices. Built on the JL7018F chipset with Bluetooth 5.4, the Assist Rx lineup offers four distinct frame styles designed to fit seamlessly into the daily lives of adults with mild-to-moderate hearing loss and age-related vision needs. At $249-$349 retail, WHSPR Assist Rx eliminates the two biggest barriers to hearing-loss intervention -- stigma and cost -- by delivering professional-grade assistance through a form factor people already wear and trust: eyeglasses.

## Features & Functions

### 1. Open-Ear Directional Stereo Speakers

**Feature:** Two directional speakers positioned at the temple tips, delivering audio through open-ear bone-adjacent projection rather than sealed ear canal insertion.

**Function:** Sound is directed toward the ear canal while allowing ambient environmental audio to pass through naturally. Unlike traditional hearing aids that seal the ear canal (creating the "occlusion effect" where the wearer's own voice sounds hollow and booming), open-ear delivery preserves the natural sound environment while layering enhanced audio on top.

**Value for George:** This is the feature that makes WHSPR feel like glasses instead of a medical device. George can wear these at his grandson's baseball game and hear the coach calling plays more clearly without losing the crack of the bat or the crowd noise that makes the experience real. There is nothing in his ear. Nothing visible. Nothing that changes how people interact with him. His daughter Sarah notices he is leaning in less and asking "What?" less -- but she cannot see anything different about him. That is the point.

### 2. Dual-Mic ANC + ENC Noise Cancellation

**Feature:** Two microphones working in concert with both Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) and Environmental Noise Cancellation (ENC) algorithms, processed on the JL7018F chipset.

**Function:** ANC reduces consistent background noise (HVAC hum, traffic drone, airplane cabin noise) by generating inverse sound waves. ENC specifically targets environmental noise during conversations and calls, isolating the human voice signal from the surrounding soundscape. Together, they perform a simplified version of what audiologists call "speech-in-noise enhancement" -- making the voice you want to hear stand out from the noise you do not.

**Value for George:** Restaurants are where George's hearing loss hurts the most. He told his audiologist he hears fine in quiet rooms, and he does. It is the clatter of dishes, the background music, the six conversations at neighboring tables -- that is what drowns out his wife Margaret across the table. ANC + ENC work together to push that noise down and bring Margaret's voice forward. George does not need a $4,000 hearing aid to solve a restaurant problem. He needs smarter audio processing at a price that respects his fixed income.

### 3. Multimodal AI Assistant ("Hey Cyan")

**Feature:** Voice-activated AI assistant powered by the JL7018F chipset, supporting ChatGPT, SparkDesk, and Doubao language models, activated by the wake word "Hey Cyan."

**Function:** The AI assistant responds to natural language voice commands for a wide range of tasks: reading text aloud from photos taken by the onboard cameras, describing scenes and objects, answering general knowledge questions, providing navigation assistance, translating languages in real-time, and setting reminders or timers. The multimodal capability means the AI can process both voice input and camera input simultaneously -- a user can point at a restaurant menu and ask "Hey Cyan, read this to me" and receive an audio readback.

**Value for George:** George does not think of himself as someone who needs "vision assistance." He thinks of himself as someone whose arms are not long enough to read the specials board anymore. The AI assistant lets him discreetly snap a photo and hear the menu read back to him without pulling out reading glasses, squinting, or asking the waiter to read it aloud. It preserves his sense of competence. He can also ask "Hey Cyan, what time does the pharmacy close?" hands-free while driving, or "Hey Cyan, what does this label say?" while grocery shopping. Every interaction is voice-in, voice-out -- no fumbling with a phone screen.

### 4. Dual Camera System (8MP / 32MP AI-Enhanced)

**Feature:** Two cameras integrated into the frame, capturing 8MP native photos (upscaled to 32MP via AI enhancement) and 1200p video at 30 frames per second, with a maximum 12-minute recording session and 40 minutes total storage.

**Function:** The dual-camera system serves three purposes. First, it powers the AI vision-assistance features (text reading, scene description, object identification). Second, it allows hands-free photo and video capture for personal documentation. Third, it provides the visual input layer for real-time AI processing -- the cameras are not just for recording, they are the AI's eyes. The 32MP AI enhancement uses computational photography to upscale and sharpen images beyond the native sensor resolution, producing clear photos even in moderate lighting.

**Value for George:** George's optometrist, Dr. Chen, demonstrates this feature during the fitting. She holds up a prescription bottle with small print and shows George how the glasses can read the dosage instructions aloud. George's reaction is immediate: "I've been using a magnifying glass for that." The camera system turns a daily frustration (reading small print on medications, receipts, utility bills, ingredient lists) into a solved problem. For George's daughter Sarah, the hands-free photo capability also means her father can capture moments with the grandchildren without fumbling with his phone.

### 5. Bluetooth 5.4 Connectivity

**Feature:** The latest Bluetooth standard (5.4), supporting LE Audio, Auracast broadcast audio, and enhanced connection stability at lower power consumption than previous Bluetooth versions.

**Function:** Bluetooth 5.4 provides three meaningful improvements over the Bluetooth 5.0/5.2 found in most hearing aids and the Bluetooth 5.3 in Ray-Ban Meta glasses. First, LE Audio delivers higher-quality audio at lower bitrates, extending battery life during music and call use. Second, Auracast compatibility enables the glasses to receive broadcast audio streams from public venues (airports, theaters, houses of worship) that deploy Auracast transmitters -- a feature that hearing aid manufacturers are racing to support but few have deployed. Third, connection stability improvements mean fewer dropped calls and less audio stuttering, particularly in crowded RF environments like shopping malls and airports.

**Value for George:** George does not know what Bluetooth version his glasses use, and he should not need to. What he knows is that his phone calls sound clear, his audiobooks do not skip, and when his church installs an Auracast loop system next year, his glasses will pick up the sermon broadcast automatically. The technical superiority manifests as reliability -- things just work. For Dr. Sharma, the audiologist recommending the device, Bluetooth 5.4 is a credibility marker: she can tell patients this is current-generation technology, not yesterday's components repackaged.

### 6. TR Thermoplastic Rubber Frame

**Feature:** Frames constructed from TR (Thermoplastic Rubber), a medical-grade polymer used in premium eyewear, offering flexibility, lightweight construction, and hypoallergenic properties.

**Function:** TR frames achieve three things that metal and standard acetate frames cannot. First, they flex under pressure without breaking -- sit on them, sleep in them accidentally, hand them to a grandchild, and they return to shape. Second, they weigh significantly less than equivalent acetate frames, reducing nose-bridge pressure during all-day wear. Third, they are hypoallergenic, eliminating the skin irritation that some patients experience with nickel-containing metal frames or certain acetate formulations. Critically for WHSPR's positioning, TR frames are prescription-ready: any optical lab can cut and fit lenses to these frames using standard equipment.

**Value for George:** George has worn glasses for 30 years. He knows what comfortable frames feel like, and he knows what cheap frames feel like. TR frames feel like his $400 Oliver Peoples -- lightweight on the nose, secure behind the ears, no red marks at the end of the day. The prescription-ready capability is essential: George wears progressive lenses. The idea of switching to non-prescription smart glasses is a nonstarter. WHSPR Assist Rx meets him where he is: his optometrist fits his exact prescription into the frame he chooses, and the result is one pair of glasses that does everything. For Dr. Emily Chen, the optometrist whitelabel partner, the frame quality is critical because it sits next to designer frames in her display case. TR gives her that confidence.

### 7. Prescription-Ready Lens System

**Feature:** All four Assist Rx models accept standard optical-lab-cut prescription lenses, including single vision, bifocal, and progressive configurations, with six lens treatment options (clear, anti-blue light, photochromic, tinted, polarized, and uncut blanks for clinic fitting).

**Function:** Unlike every other smart glasses product on the market (Ray-Ban Meta, Solos AirGo, and others all ship plano-only or with extremely limited Rx options), WHSPR Assist Rx frames are designed from the ground up to accept prescription lenses. Optical labs use the same cutting, edging, and fitting equipment they use for conventional frames. This means any optometrist or optician can order, cut, and fit lenses for WHSPR Assist Rx using their existing workflow. No special tools. No proprietary lens blanks. No manufacturer middleman.

**Value for George:** This is the feature that turns WHSPR from "an interesting gadget" into "my actual glasses." George needs progressive lenses. He cannot see the road without his distance correction and he cannot read his phone without his near correction. Any smart glasses product that requires him to choose between vision correction and hearing enhancement has already lost him. WHSPR Assist Rx is the only product that refuses to make him choose. He walks out of Dr. Chen's office with one pair of glasses: his prescription, his frame style, his lens treatment -- and hearing enhancement and AI assistance built in. The cognitive load of managing multiple pairs of glasses is gone.

### 8. Voice-Activated Hands-Free Control

**Feature:** Full device control through voice commands via the "Hey Cyan" wake word, eliminating the need for touch controls for core functions.

**Function:** Users can initiate phone calls, control music playback, activate the AI assistant, take photos, start video recording, and adjust volume using voice commands alone. The dual-mic array with ANC/ENC ensures reliable wake-word detection even in moderate-noise environments. Touch controls on the temple arm remain available as a secondary input method for users who prefer tactile interaction or are in environments where speaking aloud is inappropriate.

**Value for George:** George's hands are not as steady as they were. Small buttons and touch-sensitive surfaces are frustrating. Voice control means he can say "Hey Cyan, call Margaret" while driving without taking his eyes off the road or fumbling with his phone. He can say "Hey Cyan, take a photo" at his grandson's recital without disrupting the performance by pulling out a phone. For OrCam MyEye users paying $4,500 for similar functionality, WHSPR delivers comparable text-reading and scene-description at roughly 7% of the cost.

### 12. ANC + ENC Combined Noise Processing

**Feature:** Simultaneous Active Noise Cancellation and Environmental Noise Cancellation operating across the dual-microphone array, processed in real time on the JL7018F chipset.

**Function:** ANC and ENC are distinct systems that complement each other. ANC generates inverse sound waves to cancel steady-state noise (fan hum, engine drone, air conditioning). ENC uses beamforming algorithms to isolate the voice signal from directional noise sources during calls and conversations, ensuring the person speaking to the wearer (or on the phone) is prioritized over background chatter. Combined, they perform computational hearing enhancement -- making speech more intelligible without simply amplifying everything louder (which is the core problem with basic OTC amplifiers that George tried and returned).

**Value for George:** George tried a $79 OTC amplifier from the pharmacy. It made everything louder. The restaurant got louder. The traffic got louder. Margaret's voice got louder, but so did everything else. He returned it within a week. WHSPR's ANC + ENC approach is fundamentally different: it reduces noise while preserving speech. George does not need everything louder. He needs the right things clearer. This distinction is critical for Dr. Sharma when she explains WHSPR to patients who have had bad OTC experiences. The technology is not amplification; it is computational speech enhancement.

### 13. Magnetic 2-Pin Charging System

**Feature:** Proprietary magnetic charging connection using a 2-pin alignment system that snaps the glasses into the charging cradle without cable insertion.

**Function:** The magnetic charging system eliminates USB-C port wear, micro-alignment frustration, and cable-orientation confusion. The glasses approach the cradle and the magnets pull them into correct alignment automatically. Charging from depleted to full takes approximately 90 minutes for V03 models and approximately 110 minutes for the V06 Active.

**Value for George:** This is a small detail that matters enormously to older users. George's wife Margaret watches him fumble with the USB-C cable on his iPad every night. No asking Margaret which way the cable goes in. Accessibility in the details builds trust in the whole product.

## How To Use

### Initial Setup (10-15 Minutes)

1. **Unbox and charge.** Place the glasses on the magnetic charging cradle. Allow approximately 90 minutes for a full charge (V03) or 110 minutes (V06). The indicator LED turns solid when charging is complete.
2. **Download the companion app.** Available for iOS and Android. The app handles Bluetooth pairing, AI model selection, audio equalization preferences, firmware updates, and photo/video management.
3. **Pair via Bluetooth.** Open the app, enable Bluetooth on the phone, and follow the on-screen pairing instructions. The glasses announce "Connected" through the speakers when pairing succeeds.
4. **Set the wake word.** The default wake word is "Hey Cyan." Speak it once during setup to calibrate voice recognition to the user's vocal characteristics.
5. **Choose your AI model.** Select from ChatGPT, SparkDesk, or Doubao in the companion app. ChatGPT is the default recommendation for English-speaking users.

### Daily Use

- **Put them on.** The glasses power on automatically when unfolded (hinge sensor) or when removed from the charging cradle.
- **Hearing enhancement is always active** when the glasses are on and connected. No toggle required. ANC + ENC process audio continuously.
- **Voice commands** start with "Hey Cyan" followed by the request: "Hey Cyan, read this label." "Hey Cyan, call Margaret." "Hey Cyan, what time is it?"
- **Photos and video** can be captured via voice command ("Hey Cyan, take a photo") or via the temple-arm touch control.
- **Charging** happens at the end of the day on the magnetic cradle. Most users develop a nightstand routine identical to phone charging.

### Companion App Features

- **Audio equalization:** Adjust bass, treble, and mid-range to match patient hearing preferences. Clinicians can pre-configure these during fitting.
- **AI model management:** Switch between ChatGPT, SparkDesk, and Doubao. Configure language preferences, response verbosity, and voice speed.
- **Photo/video gallery:** View, share, and manage media captured by the onboard cameras.
- **Firmware updates:** Over-the-air updates delivered through the app to keep the glasses current with new features, bug fixes, and AI model improvements.
- **Battery monitoring:** Real-time battery level display and estimated remaining usage time.

### Clinic Fitting Workflow (For B2B Partners)

1. **Patient arrives** with existing Rx or receives a new refraction.
2. **Frame selection.** Clinician helps the patient choose from the four capsule models based on face shape, aesthetic preference, and use-case profile.
3. **Lens order.** Clinician's optical lab cuts and fits lenses to the selected frame using standard equipment. Lens treatment (anti-blue light, photochromic, etc.) is chosen during this step.
4. **Device configuration.** While lenses are being cut, the clinician or technician pairs the glasses to the patient's phone, configures the companion app, calibrates the wake word, and sets audio equalization based on the patient's audiogram or stated preferences.
5. **Patient education.** A 10-minute walkthrough covers charging, voice commands, AI assistant usage, and camera features. Printed quick-start card is included in the box.
6. **Follow-up.** Schedule a 2-week check-in (phone or in-person) to adjust audio settings based on real-world usage feedback.

## Clinical Context

### How Clinicians Should Position WHSPR Assist Rx

WHSPR Assist Rx is not a hearing aid. It is not marketed, regulated, or sold as a hearing aid. It is a smart glasses system with integrated hearing-enhancement and vision-assistance technology. This positioning is critical for three reasons:

1. **Regulatory clarity.** WHSPR Assist Rx falls under consumer electronics, not medical devices. No FDA clearance is required. No audiogram is mandated for purchase. This eliminates the regulatory overhead that slows clinic adoption of new hearing products.

2. **Patient psychology.** The patients who benefit most from WHSPR Assist Rx are the ones who have refused hearing aids. Positioning the product as "smart glasses with hearing enhancement" rather than "a hearing aid alternative" avoids triggering the stigma and denial response that prevented adoption in the first place.

3. **Complementary, not competitive.** For audiologists like Dr. Sharma, WHSPR Assist Rx captures the 40-60% of patients who decline hearing aids -- patients who currently generate zero revenue. It is not replacing hearing aid sales; it is monetizing the patients who walk out without buying anything. For optometrists like Dr. Chen, it is a natural extension of the vision-correction services they already provide, opening an entirely new revenue stream from the hearing-enhancement category.

### Patient Conversation Guide

**For patients who refuse hearing aids:**
"These are smart glasses with built-in hearing enhancement. They use noise cancellation to make conversations clearer in noisy environments. They also have an AI assistant that can read text aloud -- menus, labels, anything you point the camera at. They accept your prescription lenses. Think of them as your glasses, upgraded."

**For patients with mild-to-moderate loss:**
"Your hearing test shows mild loss in the frequencies where speech clarity matters most. That is why restaurants are harder than quiet rooms. These glasses use the same kind of noise-cancellation technology you find in premium earbuds, tuned for conversation. They will not restore clinical-grade hearing, but they will make your daily listening situations significantly easier."

**For family members (Sarah Mitchell profile):**
"Your father has been declining hearing aids for three years. These are prescription glasses with hearing enhancement built in. He will not see them as a medical device. He will see them as an upgrade to the glasses he already wears every day. The AI assistant also reads text aloud, which helps with the small-print frustration he has been having."

### Important Clinical Limitations

- WHSPR Assist Rx is designed for mild-to-moderate hearing loss. It is not appropriate for severe or profound hearing loss, where medical-grade hearing aids or cochlear implants are indicated.
- The device provides hearing enhancement through noise cancellation and speech-priority processing, not clinical amplification tuned to an audiogram. Patients with specific frequency-band losses may still need traditional hearing aids for optimal correction.
- No clinical speech-in-noise validation data (SNR improvement) is currently available. Clinicians should set patient expectations accordingly: this is a meaningful quality-of-life improvement, not a peer-reviewed clinical intervention.
- Battery life (4-5 hours active use) is shorter than dedicated hearing aids (16-31 hours). Patients who need all-day continuous hearing correction should consider hearing aids as a primary device and WHSPR as a complementary device.
- No insurance or Medicare reimbursement pathway exists for this product category. The $249-$349 retail price is entirely out-of-pocket.
