# Phase 1: Foundation & Market Intelligence

**Skill Focus:** niche-validator, buyer-persona, competitor-analysis, detailed-product-description

**Duration:** ~4-6 hours
**Dependencies:** None (this is Layer 0 -- all skills can run in parallel)

---

## Outputs Expected

### 1. Niche Validation (niche-validator)
- Elderly hearing/vision accessibility market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Comparison against traditional hearing aid market ($9B+ globally)
- OTC hearing aid regulatory landscape (FDA 2022 rule implications)
- Aging population demographic trends (65+ projected growth)
- Convergence opportunity: hearing + vision in single wearable
- Adjacent market analysis: prescription eyewear, assistive tech, telehealth accessories
- Risk assessment: regulatory, adoption barriers, clinical credibility requirements

### 2. Buyer Personas (buyer-persona)
Five personas spanning B2B and B2C tracks:

**B2B Track:**
- **Dr. Priya Sharma** -- Audiologist/Clinic Owner (primary decision-maker). Cares about patient outcomes, practice differentiation, revenue per patient, ease of integration.
- **Marcus Williams** -- Clinic Administrator/Purchasing. Cares about margin, training time, vendor support, insurance billing, inventory management.
- **Dr. Emily Chen** -- Optometrist (cross-referral partner). Cares about prescription integration, patient experience, referral revenue, clinical credibility.

**B2C Track:**
- **George Mitchell** (72, retired engineer) -- End-user with mild hearing loss + presbyopia. Cares about independence, simplicity, not looking "old," cost vs. hearing aids, technology comfort.
- **Sarah Chen** (48, daughter/caregiver) -- Proxy buyer researching for parent. Cares about parent safety, ease of setup, remote monitoring, cost justification, provider trust.

Each persona includes: demographics, psychographics, pain points, goals, decision drivers, messaging hooks, objection patterns.

### 3. Competitor Analysis (competitor-analysis)
Dual-industry competitive landscape:

**Hearing Aid Competitors:**
- Phonak (Sonova) -- Market leader, $2,000-6,000/pair, clinical distribution
- Oticon (Demant) -- Premium, strong audiologist relationships
- Starkey -- American-made, health tracking features, $1,500-5,000/pair
- Widex -- Sound quality focus, premium pricing
- ReSound (GN Audio) -- Bluetooth connectivity, Apple integration

**Smart Glasses / Assistive Tech Competitors:**
- Envision -- AI-powered glasses for visually impaired, $3,499
- Ray-Ban Meta -- Consumer smart glasses, no hearing/vision assist
- OrCam -- Text reading, face recognition, $4,500
- Vuzix -- Enterprise smart glasses, limited consumer play

**Analysis includes:** Feature matrix, pricing comparison, distribution channels, positioning gaps, WHSPR Assist advantages (price, dual function, prescription-ready, non-stigmatizing design).

### 4. Detailed Product Description (detailed-product-description)
WHSPR v1 hardware reframed through accessibility lens:
- Specs formatted for clinical credibility (not lifestyle appeal)
- Every feature mapped to an elderly user benefit
- Claims validation checklist (what can be stated vs. requires clinical evidence)
- Prescription integration specifications
- Comparison tables vs. hearing aids and vs. smart glasses
