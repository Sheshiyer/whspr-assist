## Script Overview

This is a 150-second crowdfunding campaign video for WHSPR Assist Rx -- prescription-ready smart glasses that combine hearing enhancement, AI vision assistance, and everyday eyewear. The script follows a nine-section emotional arc: hook the viewer with a universal moment of disconnection, introduce George's story, reveal the product, explain how it works, demonstrate key features, build credibility through social proof, present the offer, and close with an emotional call to action.

**Target word count for voiceover:** 370-390 words (approximately 2.5 words per second at a warm, measured pace suitable for the 55-75 primary audience and 35-55 caregiver secondary audience).

## Pre-Production Notes

### Casting

**George (Primary):**
- Male, 65-72 years old. Distinguished but approachable. Clean-shaven or neatly trimmed facial hair. Wears button-down shirts or casual polos. Looks like a retired school administrator or engineer. Not frail -- active, upright posture, clear eyes. Think Sam Waterston or Jeff Bridges energy.
- Must look natural in prescription glasses. No exaggerated squinting or cupping ear -- George is dignified, not helpless.

**Linda (George's wife):**
- Female, 63-70 years old. Warm presence. The kind of woman who sets the table with care. She looks at George with a mix of love and quiet concern. Not pitying -- observant.

**Granddaughter (Lily):**

- Female, 38-48 years old. Professional but warm. White coat optional -- a modern clinic aesthetic works better than clinical sterility. South Asian, reflecting the persona.

**Additional cast:**
- Golf buddies (2-3 males, 60-72, active and social)
- Church congregation members (diverse ages, background)
- Waiter at restaurant (any age, pleasant)

## Section 1: HOOK

**TIME CODE:** 0:00 -- 0:10
**DURATION:** 10 seconds
**EMOTIONAL BEAT:** Recognition -- "That is me. That is my father."

### Visual Direction

CAMERA: Tight close-up on George's face in soft focus. We see his eyes -- attentive, warm, slightly uncertain. He is sitting at a long dinner table. Golden evening light. Out-of-focus figures are gesturing, mouths moving, laughter evident in their body language. The audio mix tells the story: we hear what George hears. Voices are muffled. Distinct words are gone. The laughter is just a blur of sound. Forks clinking. A child's voice -- excited, rapid, indistinguishable.

CUT TO: George's hand on the table. His fingers tighten slightly around his napkin. A micro-gesture of frustration only his wife would notice.

CUT TO: George smiles. Nods. A beat too late.

### Voiceover (NARRATOR -- female, 50s, Dr. Maren register: warm, direct, unhurried)

> "He heard the laughter. He just could not catch the joke."

### On-Screen Text

`[No text. Let the image and single line land.]`

### Audio/Music

- Sound design: Muffled dinner ambience from George's perspective. Voices indistinct but warm. Fork-on-plate, glasses clinking, a child's voice rising and falling without clarity.
- Music: Solo fingerpicked acoustic guitar enters at 0:03, very low in the mix.
- The audio mix is deliberately impaired -- the audience experiences George's hearing perspective.

### Director Notes

This opening must be felt, not explained. The audience should recognize this moment instantly -- either as their own experience or as something they have watched a parent go through. The late smile is the key performance beat. George is not sad. He is compensating. That distinction matters.

## Post-Production Notes

### Color Grade
- Warm throughout. Slightly desaturated highlights, warm shadows. Think Kodak Gold 200 film stock. Not orange, not flat -- warm and inviting.
- Clinic scenes slightly cooler (neutral white balance) to suggest professionalism, but never clinical blue.
- Golden hour for the dinner table scenes (hook and CTA) and the morning bedside scene.

### Sound Mix
- Voiceover at -6dB (standard narration level).
- Music at -18dB under narration, -12dB during visual-only moments.
- Sound design (ambient, foley) at -15dB under narration.
- The "muffled hearing" effect in the hook should be created by rolling off frequencies above 2kHz and adding a subtle room reverb. It should feel like listening through a wall, not like bad audio.

### Aspect Ratios Needed
- 16:9 (YouTube, website hero, Kickstarter page)
- 9:16 (Instagram Stories, TikTok, short-form vertical)
- 1:1 (Facebook/Instagram feed)
- Each ratio requires re-framing, not just cropping. Key action must remain centered in every aspect ratio.

### Deliverables
1. Full 150-second video (16:9, 4K)
2. 60-second cutdown (hook + product reveal + offer + CTA)
3. 30-second cutdown (hook + product reveal + CTA)
4. 15-second cutdown (hook + price comparison + CTA)
5. 6-second bumper (tagline + logo + URL)
