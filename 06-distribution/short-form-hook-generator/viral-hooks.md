## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Voice Direction for Short-Form

Dr. Maren's voice in short-form content runs slightly warmer and more energetic than the long-form campaign materials. The register stays the same -- trusted clinician, never salesperson -- but the pacing is tighter and the opening line must land within the first 2 seconds. No throat-clearing. No preamble. First word matters.

**Pacing guide:**
- 0:00-0:03 -- Hook line (stop the scroll)
- 0:03-0:09 -- Product reveal and core benefit
- 0:09-0:13 -- Key differentiator (price, form factor, or feature)
- 0:13-0:15 -- Tagline + CTA

**Delivery style:** Think a trusted friend leaning across the table to tell you something useful. Not a pitch. A recommendation.

### Platform Optimization

| Platform | Aspect | Audio | Captions | CTA Style |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 9:16 | Sound-on priority, works sound-off with text | Auto-captions + branded overlays | "Link in bio" + comment pinning |
| Instagram Reels | 9:16 | Sound-on priority, works sound-off with text | Branded text overlays | "Link in bio" or swipe CTA |
| YouTube Shorts | 9:16 | Sound-on priority | Burned-in captions, large text | Subscribe + link in description |
| Facebook Reels | 9:16 or 1:1 | Sound-off design, sound-on enhancement | Large burned-in captions | "Learn more" link overlay |

- Yes: hearing enhancement, smart glasses, prescription-ready, hear more clearly, your audiologist, clarity, connection, designed for your life

### A/B Testing Recommendations

**Test 1 -- Hook Category Performance:**
- Run one hook from each category simultaneously (NW-01, CS-01, OB-01, VO-01)
- Metric: 3-second view rate (scroll-stop effectiveness)
- Hypothesis: VO-01 (Visual Oddity) wins on scroll-stop; OB-01 (Outcome) wins on completion rate

**Test 2 -- Audience Targeting:**
- Sarah-facing hooks (NW-03, OB-04) vs George-facing hooks (NW-02, OB-01)
- Metric: Click-through rate to waitlist
- Hypothesis: Sarah-facing hooks produce higher CTR because she is the active researcher

**Test 3 -- Soundbyte Anchors:**
- "Not a hearing aid. Your glasses." (CS-01) vs "Glasses that listen." (VO-01) vs "$299 vs $3,200" (CS-04)
- Metric: Share rate
- Hypothesis: "$299 vs $3,200" drives highest share rate (concrete, surprising value proposition)

**Test 4 -- Opening Frame:**
- Text-first hooks (NW-01, CS-01) vs Visual-first hooks (VO-01, VO-02) vs People-first hooks (OB-01, OB-04)
- Metric: 1-second hold rate
- Hypothesis: Visual-first hooks win on 1-second hold; People-first hooks win on 15-second completion
