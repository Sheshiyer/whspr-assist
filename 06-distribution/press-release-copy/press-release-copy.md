# WHSPR Assist Launches Prescription Smart Glasses That Combine Hearing Enhancement, AI Vision Assistance, and Everyday Eyewear for Under $350

**First-of-its-kind device addresses the $20 billion hearing aid market's biggest barrier: 80 percent of Americans with hearing loss use nothing at all**

---

**SAN FRANCISCO, February 2026** -- WHSPR Assist today announced the launch of WHSPR Assist Rx, the first prescription-ready smart glasses to combine hearing enhancement, AI-powered vision assistance, and everyday eyewear in a single frame. Priced from $249, WHSPR Assist Rx is designed for the more than 53 million Americans with hearing loss -- 80 percent of whom currently use no hearing device at all -- and will be distributed primarily through audiologists and optometrists nationwide.

The product addresses a structural gap in hearing healthcare. Despite a global hearing aid market exceeding $20 billion annually, adoption rates remain critically low. The average delay between hearing aid candidacy and first adoption is 8.9 years. The three primary barriers -- stigma, cost, and access -- have persisted for decades. WHSPR Assist Rx eliminates all three: it looks like everyday prescription glasses, costs 90 to 95 percent less than traditional hearing aids, and is fitted and calibrated by the same healthcare professionals patients already trust.

"We built WHSPR Assist Rx because the hearing industry has been solving the wrong problem," said the company's founder. "The technology to help people hear better has existed for years. What hasn't existed is a form factor that people are willing to wear. Ninety-two percent of adults over 65 already wear glasses every day. We put the hearing technology inside the glasses they already reach for each morning."

## Product Overview: WHSPR Assist Rx

WHSPR Assist Rx is built on the JL7018F chipset platform and delivers three core capabilities in a lightweight TR (Thermoplastic Rubber) frame that accepts standard prescription lenses from any optical laboratory.

### Hearing Enhancement

- Open-ear directional speakers in both temple arms deliver enhanced audio without occluding the ear canal
- Dual-microphone system with Active Noise Cancellation (ANC) and Environmental Noise Cancellation (ENC) suppresses background noise and isolates speech
- Audiogram-based hearing profile calibration provides personalized frequency shaping matched to the individual's hearing curve -- not generic amplification
- Companion app enables clinical calibration by audiologists or guided self-calibration by the wearer

### AI Vision Assistance

- Dual camera system: 8MP standard and 32MP AI-enhanced photography
- 1200p video capture at 30 frames per second
- Multimodal AI integration for text recognition, scene description, and environmental awareness
- Reads printed text aloud -- menus, medication labels, street signs, mail -- through the open-ear speakers
- "Hey Cyan" voice activation for hands-free camera control

### Connectivity and Comfort

- Bluetooth 5.4 for stable, low-power connection to iOS and Android devices
- Temple touch gesture controls for volume, playback, and camera operation
- Magnetic 2-pin snap charging -- no fumbling with small batteries or charging cases
- TR thermoplastic frames: lightweight, flexible, hypoallergenic
- Approximately 4 hours call time and 5 hours audio playback per charge

### Four Frame Styles

All models are available in matte black and clear/transparent. The Active model includes sport-tinted, polarized, and photochromic lens options.

## Distribution: Healthcare Professionals First

WHSPR Assist Rx is distributed primarily through audiologists and optometrists -- not electronics retailers. This clinical delivery model is a deliberate strategic choice.

Healthcare professionals fit the frame, install the appropriate prescription lens through their optical laboratory, and calibrate the hearing profile using the companion app's Provider Portal. Patients receive a device that has been personalized to their specific hearing and vision needs by a clinician they trust.

The companion app's Provider Portal gives clinicians ongoing visibility into patient usage data, hearing trends, and device performance. This transforms a single transaction into a continuing care relationship -- an outcome that consumer electronics purchased online cannot deliver.

WHSPR Assist also offers a whitelabel program for clinic chains and healthcare groups, providing custom-branded hardware and a skinned companion app under the partner's own practice identity.

## About WHSPR Assist

WHSPR Assist is an assistive wearable technology company building prescription-ready smart glasses that combine hearing enhancement, AI vision assistance, and everyday eyewear. Founded on the principle that no one should miss a conversation because of stigma or cost, WHSPR Assist delivers its products through trusted healthcare professionals -- audiologists and optometrists -- who fit, calibrate, and support each device as part of an ongoing care relationship.

The company's flagship product, WHSPR Assist Rx, is designed for adults aged 55 to 75 with mild-to-moderate hearing loss who already wear prescription glasses. By integrating open-ear directional audio with ANC/ENC noise processing, audiogram-based frequency shaping, and a 32MP AI camera system into a lightweight TR frame, WHSPR Assist Rx addresses the hearing, vision, and independence needs of an underserved population of more than 53 million Americans -- at a fraction of the cost of traditional hearing aids.

WHSPR Assist is headquartered in San Francisco, California.
