## 1. Response Philosophy

Every review -- five stars or one star -- is a conversation with a real person. The person behind the review made a decision to trust WHSPR Assist with their hearing, their vision, and their dignity. Our response must honor that trust, even when the review is harsh.

**Three principles govern every response:**

1. **Dignity first.** The reviewer is a competent adult. We never talk down, deflect, or minimize their experience.
2. **Ownership always.** When something went wrong, we own it. No hedging, no corporate language, no "we apologize for any inconvenience."
3. **Offline resolution.** Negative reviews get resolved in private. The public response shows empathy and moves the conversation to care@whspr.com. We never debate in the comments.

**Response voice:** Dr. Maren -- the trusted optometrist who speaks to people the way she would speak to her own family. Direct, warm, free of jargon, never condescending. She recommends. She does not sell. She does not hype. She explains.

## 2. Word Rules for Review Responses

### Always Use
- "hear more clearly" not "hearing loss solution"
- "see more sharply" not "vision impairment aid"
- "smart glasses" not "device" or "hearing aid alternative"
- "your audiologist" or "your optometrist" not "a doctor"
- "enhancement" not "correction" or "treatment"
- "straightforward" not "easy" or "simple"

### Never Use in Any Review Response
- hearing loss solution
- disability
- elderly / senior
- patient (when referring to the reviewer)
- device (use "glasses" or "smart glasses")
- easy / simple
- game-changing / revolutionary
- "we apologize for any inconvenience" (say what you actually mean)
- "per our policy" (people are not policies)

## 3. Five-Star Response Strategy

### Goal
Thank by name. Reinforce the specific benefit they mentioned. Invite them to share their experience with others.

### Framework

```
1. Thank [Name] by name
2. Reflect back the specific benefit they described (hearing clarity, comfort, AI features, etc.)
3. Express genuine warmth -- not corporate gratitude
4. Include a soft CTA: share with someone who might benefit, or connect with their audiologist
5. Close with the brand spirit
```

### Tone Calibration
- Warmth: 9/10
- Formality: 3/10
- Length: 3-5 sentences
- Energy: Calm, genuine, grateful

### Rules
- Never copy-paste the same response across reviews. Each response references the specific detail the reviewer shared.
- Never use exclamation points excessively. One is the maximum.
- Never ask for a referral directly. Frame it as sharing with someone who might benefit.
- Always feel like a person wrote it, not a template engine.

## 4. One-Star Response Strategy

### Goal
Acknowledge the frustration. Own the problem. Move offline to resolve it. Protect the brand publicly without being defensive.

### Framework

```
1. Thank [Name] for the honest feedback
2. Acknowledge their specific frustration (do not generalize)
3. Own it -- no deflection, no "but actually"
4. Provide a clear next step: care@whspr.com with their order details
5. Express commitment to making it right
6. Close with a personal note -- not a corporate sign-off
```

### Tone Calibration
- Warmth: 8/10
- Formality: 4/10
- Length: 4-6 sentences
- Energy: Steady, empathetic, unhurried
- Apologetic register: Slightly sorry, genuinely caring, never groveling

### Rules
- Never argue with the reviewer. Even if they are factually wrong about a feature, the public response is not the place to correct them.
- Never promise a specific resolution publicly (refund, replacement). Handle that offline.
- Never blame the reviewer ("you may have set it up incorrectly").
- Never reference other negative reviews or patterns ("we've been hearing this a lot").
- Always move the conversation to care@whspr.com within the first 3-4 sentences.
- If the complaint is about a known limitation (battery life, not replacing hearing aids), be honest about it.

### Known Limitation Responses

When a review touches a known limitation, we do not pretend it does not exist. We acknowledge it honestly and explain what we are doing about it.

**Battery Life (4-5 hours):**
We know the battery does not last all day. We designed WHSPR Assist for the situations that matter most -- dinner out, a phone call, a morning walk -- and most users find 4-5 hours covers those moments. The magnetic charger refills in about an hour. Our next generation is targeting all-day battery. In the meantime, we want to make sure you are getting the most out of each charge.

**Not a Hearing Aid Replacement (for severe loss):**
WHSPR Assist is designed for mild-to-moderate hearing enhancement. It is not a substitute for prescription hearing aids, and we are always honest about that. We recommend speaking with your audiologist for the best experience. If your hearing needs have changed, your audiologist can help determine the right next step.

**New Brand / Trust Concerns:**
We understand that trusting a new brand with something as important as your hearing takes courage. The technology inside -- dual-mic noise cancellation, Bluetooth 5.4, AI vision processing -- is built on proven components used across professional audio and imaging. But the real trust signal is your audiologist or optometrist recommending these. They stake their reputation on every product in their practice.

## 5. Troll and Malicious Review Strategy

### Identification Criteria

A review may be malicious or fraudulent if it meets two or more of these signals:

1. **No verified purchase** (on platforms that show this)
2. **Generic language** that does not reference any specific product feature or experience
3. **Profile pattern** -- the reviewer has left multiple 1-star reviews across unrelated products in a short period
4. **Competitor mention** -- the review promotes a competing product by name
5. **Profanity or personal attacks** directed at the brand or team
6. **Factual impossibility** -- describes a feature or experience that does not exist in the product
7. **Timing pattern** -- multiple negative reviews appear within hours of each other from new accounts

### Response Decision Tree

```
Is it a verified purchase?
  YES --> Respond with standard 1-star framework (it is a real customer)
  NO  --> Does it contain specific product details?
            YES --> Respond with standard 1-star framework (benefit of the doubt)
            NO  --> Does it violate platform review guidelines?
                      YES --> Report to platform AND post a brief, dignified response
                      NO  --> Post a brief, dignified response only
```

### Troll Response Framework

```
1. Keep it short (2-3 sentences maximum)
2. Do not engage with specific claims that cannot be verified
3. Invite them to reach out to care@whspr.com if they have a genuine experience to discuss
4. Do not mention reporting or flagging in the public response
5. Report separately through the platform's review reporting system
```

### Tone Calibration for Troll Responses
- Warmth: 5/10 (professional, not cold)
- Formality: 6/10 (slightly more formal than genuine reviews)
- Length: 2-3 sentences maximum
- Energy: Neutral, unbothered, confident

### When to Report vs. Respond

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Unverified purchase, generic attack | Report + brief response |
| Verified purchase, harsh but specific | Full 1-star response (treat as genuine) |
| Promotes competitor by name | Report + brief response |
| Contains profanity or slurs | Report + no response (let platform handle) |
| Factually impossible claims | Report + brief clarifying response |
| Multiple coordinated negative reviews | Report all + respond to one, briefly |

## 7. Platform-Specific Guidance

### Amazon
- Respond within 24 hours
- Keep responses under 1,000 characters (Amazon may truncate)
- Use "we" not "I" for brand consistency
- Do not include links (Amazon strips them) -- direct to care@whspr.com instead
- Monitor for "Verified Purchase" badges to identify genuine vs. potentially fraudulent reviews

### Google Business
- Respond within 48 hours
- Can be slightly longer than Amazon responses
- Use the business name in closing ("The WHSPR Assist Team")
- Thank reviewers for visiting the clinic or ordering through a provider when applicable

### Trustpilot
- Respond within 48 hours
- Mark genuine negative reviews as "Invited" or "Organic" for transparency
- Report reviews that violate Trustpilot guidelines through their reporting tool
- Reference the verification process if a review appears fraudulent

### Audiologist / Clinic Review Platforms
- Coordinate response with the prescribing clinic when the review mentions a specific provider
- Never respond on behalf of a clinic without their approval
- Offer to connect the reviewer with the clinic directly if the issue involves fitting or prescription

## 8. Escalation Protocol

### Level 1: Standard Response (90% of reviews)
- Handled by the review response team using this playbook
- Response within 24-48 hours depending on platform
- No leadership involvement required

### Level 2: Product Safety or Defect Mention (8% of reviews)
- Flagged immediately to product team
- Response within 12 hours
- Must include care@whspr.com escalation path
- Product team reviews for pattern analysis

### Level 3: Legal, Medical Claim, or Coordinated Attack (2% of reviews)
- Flagged to leadership and legal
- Response drafted by communications lead
- Platform-level reporting initiated
- Pattern analysis across all platforms
