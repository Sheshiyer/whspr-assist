### 1.2 Target Niches

**Niche 1: Active Aging / Living Well After 50**
- Creators who document life after 50 with confidence, humor, and style
- Content themes: fitness over 50, retirement lifestyle, travel for experienced adults, cooking, gardening, grandparenting
- Platform skew: Instagram (primary), YouTube (secondary), TikTok (emerging)
- Example content: "Morning routine at 62" / "What I wish I knew about retirement" / "Style after 50"

**Niche 2: Caregiver Communities**
- Adult children (35-55) who share caregiver journeys with honesty
- Content themes: parent care tips, communication with aging parents, gift ideas for parents, health tech reviews for family
- Platform skew: TikTok (primary), Instagram (secondary), YouTube (long-form)
- Example content: "Things I got my dad that he actually uses" / "Having the hearing conversation with your parent"

**Niche 3: Audiology & Optometry Professionals**
- Licensed audiologists and optometrists who create educational content
- Content themes: hearing health education, vision care tips, product reviews, myth-busting, patient stories
- Platform skew: Instagram (primary), TikTok (secondary), LinkedIn (B2B amplification)
- Example content: "What your audiologist wants you to know" / "The truth about OTC hearing devices"

**Niche 4: Assistive Tech & Accessibility Reviewers**
- Tech reviewers who specialize in wearable technology and assistive innovation
- Content themes: smart glasses reviews, wearable tech roundups, accessibility tools, health tech deep dives
- Platform skew: YouTube (primary), TikTok (secondary), Instagram (tertiary)
- Example content: "Best smart glasses of 2026" / "Tech that actually helps people"

**Niche 5: AARP-Adjacent Lifestyle**
- Lifestyle creators whose audience skews 45-70 and values substance over trends
- Content themes: home organization, wellness routines, financial planning after retirement, quality-of-life upgrades
- Platform skew: Facebook (primary), Instagram (secondary), YouTube (secondary)
- Example content: "Products that improved my daily life" / "What I spend on health after 60"

### 1.3 Influencer Qualification Criteria

Before outreach, every influencer must pass these five checks:

1. **Audience Alignment:** 60%+ of their audience falls within our target demographics (adults 45-75 or caregivers 35-55)
2. **Engagement Quality:** Engagement rate above 3% (micro), 2% (mid), 1.5% (macro). Comments must show real conversation, not bots.
3. **Brand Safety:** No content that mocks aging, uses pity framing, promotes medical misinformation, or conflicts with dignity-first positioning
4. **Content Tone:** Aligns with WHSPR voice -- calm, informed, respectful. No clickbait, no fear tactics, no breathless hype.
5. **Platform Compliance:** No history of undisclosed sponsorships or FTC violations

## 4. Follow-Up Sequence

### Cadence Overview

| Step | Timing | Channel | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| **Step 1: Initial Outreach** | Day 0 | DM or Email (platform-native) | Introduce brand, establish relevance, make the ask |
| **Step 2: Warm Bump** | Day 3 | Same channel as initial | Gentle follow-up. Add value (press link, data point). Do not repeat the pitch. |
| **Step 3: Final Touch** | Day 7 | Same channel | Last touch. Respect their time. Leave the door open. No guilt. |

### Follow-Up Principles

- Never follow up more than twice after the initial outreach (3 total touches maximum)
- Each follow-up must add new value -- a stat, a link, a relevant piece of content -- not just "checking in"
- If they do not respond after the third touch, archive and revisit in 90 days
- If they respond with "not now," mark for follow-up in 60 days with a fresh angle
- Never use urgency language ("limited spots," "offer expires"). That violates brand voice.

## 5. Outreach Dos and Don'ts

### Always Do

- Research the creator thoroughly before reaching out. Reference specific content.
- Personalize every message. Templates are starting points, not finished copy.
- Lead with why their audience needs this, not why the brand needs them.
- Respect their creative independence. No scripts. No mandatory talking points.
- Be transparent about the commercial relationship (affiliate, gifted product).
- Use the brand voice: calm, professional, peer-to-peer. Never salesy.
- Follow FTC disclosure guidelines and remind creators of #ad / #sponsored requirements.

### Never Do

- Send mass-blast identical messages. Every DM and email must be personalized.
- Use the words: elderly, senior, old, disability, hearing loss, anti-aging, game-changing, revolutionary.
- Promise medical outcomes or hearing aid equivalence.
- Use urgency or scarcity tactics ("limited spots," "offer expires tomorrow").
- Pitch creators whose content conflicts with dignity-first brand positioning.
- Follow up more than twice. Three touches maximum. Then archive.
- Ask for editorial control or pre-approval of their content.
- Offer payment in exchange for positive reviews. The offer is product + affiliate commission for honest content.

## 7. Creator Brief (Shared After Agreement)

Once a creator confirms interest, send this brief alongside the product. This is NOT a script -- it is background information to help them create accurate, informed content.

### What WHSPR Assist Is

- Prescription-ready smart glasses with built-in hearing enhancement and AI vision assistance
- $249-$349 retail
- Dual-microphone directional audio that isolates speech in noisy environments
- 32MP AI camera that reads text, describes scenes, and recognizes faces
- Bluetooth 5.4 for hands-free calls and audio streaming
- Available through audiologists, optometrists, and direct

### What WHSPR Assist Is Not

- Not a hearing aid. Not positioned as one. Not a replacement for one.
- Not a medical device. It is a consumer wearable with hearing enhancement features.
- Not just for people with hearing difficulties. Anyone who wants clearer audio in noisy environments benefits.

### Language We Prefer

- "Smart glasses with hearing enhancement" (not "hearing aid alternative")
- "Hear more clearly" (not "fix hearing loss")
- "Designed for your life" (not "designed for seniors")
- "Enhancement" (not "treatment" or "correction")

### Language We Ask Creators to Avoid

- Elderly, senior, old, disability, impairment, anti-aging
- "Hearing loss solution" or "cures hearing loss"
- Fear-based language about hearing decline
- Pity framing ("you deserve to hear again")
- Medical claims or hearing aid equivalence

### FTC Compliance Reminder

All sponsored content must include clear disclosure per FTC guidelines. Use #ad or #sponsored in a visible location. If the product was gifted, disclose that as well. We support full transparency.
