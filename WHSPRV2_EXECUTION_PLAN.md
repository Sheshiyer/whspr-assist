# WHSPR Assist -- Master Execution Plan

**Brand:** WHSPR Assist (Prescription Smart Glasses for Elderly Accessibility)
**Scenario:** Dual-Track B2B/B2C Campaign
**Date:** 10 February 2026
**Total Skills:** 14+ (across 6 phases)
**Total Phases:** 6 (4 core + 2 optional)
**Estimated Time:** 20-30 hours

---

## Overview

This execution plan breaks down the WHSPR Assist brand launch into **6 phases**. The first 4 phases are core, with feedback checkpoints between each. Phases 5 and 6 are optional extensions for email, trade shows, and whitelabel packaging.

Unlike WHSPR v1 (single consumer track), WHSPR Assist runs **dual B2B and B2C tracks** throughout, roughly doubling the copy and positioning work. The B2B track targets clinicians and clinic administrators. The B2C track targets end-users (elderly 50+) and their family caregivers.

**Key difference from v1:** Same hardware, completely different market positioning. Every skill output must be framed through the accessibility and clinical lens, not lifestyle or content creation.

## Phase Breakdown

### PHASE 1: Foundation & Market Intelligence
**Duration:** ~4-6 hours
**Output Type:** Research, Validation, & Personas

```
1. niche-validator
2. buyer-persona
3. competitor-analysis
4. detailed-product-description
```

**What happens:**
- **niche-validator**: Validate the elderly hearing/vision accessibility market. Compare against traditional hearing aid market ($9B+). Assess OTC hearing aid regulations (FDA 2022), aging population demographics, and the convergence of hearing + vision in a single wearable. Validate against adjacent markets: prescription eyewear, assistive technology, telehealth accessories.
- **buyer-persona**: Generate 5 detailed personas spanning both B2B and B2C tracks:
  - **Dr. Priya Sharma** (Audiologist/Clinic Owner) -- B2B primary decision-maker
  - **George Mitchell** (72, retired engineer) -- B2C end-user with mild hearing loss + presbyopia
  - **Sarah Chen** (48, daughter/caregiver) -- B2C proxy buyer researching for parent
  - **Marcus Williams** (Clinic Administrator/Purchasing) -- B2B operational buyer
  - **Dr. Emily Chen** (Optometrist) -- B2B cross-referral partner
- **competitor-analysis**: Map the competitive landscape across TWO industries:
  - **Hearing aids:** Phonak, Oticon, Starkey, Widex, ReSound (traditional, $1,000-6,000/pair)
  - **Smart glasses:** Envision, Ray-Ban Meta, OrCam, Vuzix (tech-forward, varying price)
  - Identify the gap: no product combines hearing + vision + prescription-ready at $199-249
- **detailed-product-description**: Reframe the V03/V06 hardware specs through the accessibility lens. Same JL7018F chipset, 1200p dual camera, TR thermoplastic frames, open-ear ANC/ENC audio, Bluetooth 5.4 -- but every feature description must speak to elderly needs, clinical credibility, and caregiver peace of mind.

**Output files:**
- Market validation report (niche-validator)
- 5 persona documents (buyer-persona)
- Competitive landscape matrix (competitor-analysis)
- Accessibility-framed product description (detailed-product-description)

**CHECKPOINT 1: Review Phase 1 Outputs**
```
Questions for feedback:
  - Is the market validation compelling? Does the TAM/SAM/SOM make sense?
  - Are the 5 personas accurate to the B2B/B2C split?
  - Is the competitive landscape complete? Any missing players?
  - Does the product description successfully reframe specs for accessibility?
  - Are there regulatory considerations we missed?

Ready to proceed -> PHASE 2? (yes/no)
```

### PHASE 2: Positioning & Voice Strategy
**Duration:** ~4-6 hours
**Output Type:** Strategy, Positioning, & Brand Voice

```
1. product-positioning-summary
2. mds-messaging-direction-summary
3. voice-and-tone
```

**What happens:**
- **product-positioning-summary**: Build a CBBE (Customer-Based Brand Equity) framework for WHSPR Assist as an assistive wearable. Position against both hearing aids (we are lighter, cheaper, multi-function) and smart glasses (we are medically credible, prescription-ready, designed for seniors). Create segment-specific positioning for each of the 5 personas.
- **mds-messaging-direction-summary**: Develop dual-track messaging pillars:
  - **B2B Track:** Clinical efficacy, patient outcomes, practice revenue, easy integration, insurance/reimbursement potential, training support
  - **B2C Track:** Independence, dignity, simplicity, family connection, affordability vs. hearing aids, "looks like regular glasses"
  - Core message hierarchy, proof points, objection handling for each track
- **voice-and-tone**: Define a SPLIT voice system:
  - **B2B Voice (Clinical Peer):** Authoritative, evidence-based, peer-to-peer with clinicians. Think JAMA meets product brief. Data-driven, respectful of clinical expertise.
  - **B2C Voice (Compassionate Companion):** Warm, reassuring, dignified. Never patronizing. Speaks to capability not disability. Addresses the caregiver with empathy.

**Output files:**
- CBBE positioning framework (product-positioning-summary)
- Dual-track messaging document (mds-messaging-direction-summary)
- Split voice and tone guidelines (voice-and-tone)

**CHECKPOINT 2: Review Phase 2 Outputs**
```
Questions for feedback:
  - Does the CBBE positioning differentiate from both hearing aids AND smart glasses?
  - Are the B2B messaging pillars credible to clinicians?
  - Are the B2C messaging pillars emotionally resonant without being patronizing?
  - Does the clinical peer voice feel authentic?
  - Does the compassionate companion voice maintain dignity?

Ready to proceed -> PHASE 3? (yes/no)
```

### PHASE 3: Copy & Campaign Creative
**Duration:** ~8-10 hours
**Output Type:** Landing Pages, Ad Copy, & Video Scripts

```
1. campaign-page-copy (B2B + B2C)
2. pre-launch-ads (B2B + B2C)
3. campaign-video-script
```

**What happens:**
- **campaign-page-copy**: Generate TWO separate landing pages:
  - **B2B Landing Page (Clinician):** Hero with clinical authority, patient outcome data, integration workflow, pricing/margin calculator, demo request CTA, compliance and certification badges, testimonial from pilot clinic
  - **B2C Landing Page (End-User/Caregiver):** Hero with emotional independence message, feature benefits in plain language, comparison vs. hearing aids (cost, comfort, stigma), family testimonial, "Find a provider near you" CTA, FAQ addressing senior concerns
- **pre-launch-ads**: Generate ads for BOTH tracks:
  - **B2B Ads:** LinkedIn sponsored content, trade publication ads (Hearing Review, Audiology Today), Google Search (audiologist + hearing aid alternative queries), email ad inserts for clinic newsletters
  - **B2C Ads:** Meta ads targeting 50+ demographics and caregiver audiences, Google Search (hearing aid cost, hearing aid alternative, smart glasses for seniors), YouTube pre-roll for health/wellness content
- **campaign-video-script**: Generate TWO video concepts:
  - **Clinical Demo (2 min):** Product walkthrough for clinicians. Prescription fitting process, patient onboarding, app configuration, hearing enhancement demo, vision assist features. Professional, informative, efficient.
  - **Emotional Consumer (60 sec):** Story of a grandparent reconnecting with family. Subtle -- shows the glasses enabling conversation at dinner, reading to grandchild, walking confidently outdoors. Never shows disability, always shows capability. Ends with tagline: "Hear more. See more. Live more."

**Output files:**
- B2B landing page copy (campaign-page-copy)
- B2C landing page copy (campaign-page-copy)
- B2B ad variants -- LinkedIn, trade pubs, Google (pre-launch-ads)
- B2C ad variants -- Meta 50+, Google, YouTube (pre-launch-ads)
- Clinical demo video script (campaign-video-script)
- Emotional consumer video script (campaign-video-script)

**CHECKPOINT 3: Review Phase 3 Outputs**
```
Questions for feedback:
  - Does the B2B landing page establish clinical credibility?
  - Does the B2C landing page resonate emotionally without being patronizing?
  - Are the B2B ads appropriate for LinkedIn and trade publications?
  - Are the B2C ads effective for Meta 50+ targeting?
  - Does the clinical demo script cover the right features for clinicians?
  - Does the emotional consumer script maintain dignity and warmth?

Ready to proceed -> PHASE 4? (yes/no)

### PHASE 4: Press & Authority
**Duration:** ~3-4 hours
**Output Type:** Press Release & Clinical Brief

```
1. press-release-copy
2. clinical-validation-brief
```

**What happens:**
- **press-release-copy**: Generate a press release framed for MedTech and hearing health publications (NOT lifestyle/tech press like v1). Angle: "WHSPR Assist bridges the gap between hearing aids and smart glasses, offering the first affordable prescription-ready dual hearing/vision enhancement wearable." Target outlets: Hearing Review, Audiology Today, MedTech Dive, FierceBiotech, AARP publications. Include boilerplate, key statistics, and clinical credibility markers.
- **clinical-validation-brief**: A NEW deliverable not in v1. This document covers:
  - FDA regulatory pathway analysis (OTC hearing aid classification under FDA 2022 rule)
  - What claims WHSPR Assist can and cannot make without clinical trials
  - Framework for a pilot clinical study (sample size, methodology, endpoints)
  - Insurance and reimbursement landscape (Medicare, Medicaid, private)
  - Compliance checklist (FCC, FDA, HIPAA if app stores health data)
  - Recommended certifications and partnerships (AAA endorsement path)

**Output files:**
- MedTech press release (press-release-copy)
- Press release variants (hearing health, aging/AARP, technology)
- Clinical validation and regulatory brief (clinical-validation-brief)

**CHECKPOINT 4: Review Phase 4 Outputs**
```
Questions for feedback:
  - Is the press release positioned correctly for MedTech (not lifestyle)?
  - Are the regulatory claims accurate and conservative?
  - Is the clinical study framework realistic and actionable?
  - Does the insurance/reimbursement analysis cover the right codes?
  - Ready to move into optional phases?

Review complete!

### PHASE 6: Whitelabel Package
**Duration:** Custom (no brandmint skill -- manual document creation)
**Output Type:** 6 Whitelabel Documents

This phase has no corresponding brandmint skill. It produces 6 standalone documents for shadow whitelabel partnerships:

1. **Partner Program Overview** -- Tiers (Silver/Gold/Platinum), margin structure, territory rights, minimum orders
2. **Product & App Skinning Guide** -- How partners customize the hardware (engraving, color) and app (logo, colors, splash screen)
3. **Clinic Deployment Guide** -- Implementation playbook for clinic staff (fitting, onboarding, troubleshooting, patient handoff)
4. **Co-Branding Guidelines** -- What partners can and cannot do with the WHSPR Assist brand in their materials
5. **API Integration Spec** -- Technical documentation for partners integrating WHSPR Assist data into their clinic management systems
6. **Partner Marketing Kit** -- Ready-to-use assets (email templates, social posts, print ads) that partners customize with their branding

## Dependency Map

Skills are organized into dependency layers. Skills within the same layer can run in parallel.

```
Layer 0 (Parallel):    niche-validator, buyer-persona, competitor-analysis
    |
    v
Layer 1 (Parallel):    detailed-product-description, product-positioning-summary
    |
    v
Layer 2:               mds-messaging-direction-summary
    |
    v
Layer 3:               voice-and-tone
    |
    v
Layer 4 (Parallel):    campaign-page-copy (B2B + B2C), pre-launch-ads (B2B + B2C)
    |
    v
Layer 5 (Parallel):    campaign-video-script, press-release-copy, clinical-validation-brief
```

**What this means:**
- Phases 1-4 are sequential (each depends on prior outputs)
- Within each phase, skills can often run in parallel
- B2B and B2C variants within a skill can be generated in parallel
- Total estimated time: **20-30 hours** for all core skills (more than v1 due to dual tracks)
