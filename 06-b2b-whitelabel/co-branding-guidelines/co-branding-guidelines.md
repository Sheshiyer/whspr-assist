# WHSPR Assist Co-Branding Guidelines

## 1. Purpose

These guidelines govern how whitelabel partners represent the WHSPR Assist brand alongside their own in all marketing materials, packaging, digital assets, and patient-facing communications. Adherence to these guidelines is a contractual requirement for all partner tiers and is reviewed during quarterly business reviews.

All partner-created materials that reference WHSPR Assist, its products, or its technology must be submitted for approval before publication or distribution. Approval requests are submitted through the partner portal; turnaround is 3 business days for standard requests and 5 business days for materials requiring legal review.

---

## 2. Logo Lockups

### 2.1 Co-Branded Format

The standard co-branded logo format is:

**[Partner Name] x WHSPR Assist**

This lockup is used on all co-branded materials where both brands appear together. The "x" connector is lowercase, set in the same typeface as the surrounding text, with a single space on each side.

### 2.2 Lockup Specifications

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Partner logo | Partner's standard horizontal logo mark |
| Connector | Lowercase "x" in the body typeface, optically centered between logos |
| WHSPR Assist logo | WHSPR Assist horizontal word mark (provided in brand asset kit) |
| Minimum size | WHSPR Assist logo must be no smaller than 20mm wide in print or 80px wide on screen |
| Clear space | Minimum clear space equal to the height of the "W" in WHSPR on all sides of the combined lockup |
| Orientation | Horizontal only; vertical stacking is not permitted for the co-branded lockup |

### 2.3 Logo Usage by Tier

| Tier | Primary Lockup | WHSPR Assist Logo Visibility |
|------|---------------|------------------------------|
| Silver | "[Partner Name] x WHSPR Assist" | Full co-branded lockup on all materials |
| Gold | "[Partner Name] x WHSPR Assist" or partner logo with "featuring WHSPR Assist technology" tagline | WHSPR Assist may appear as secondary brand |
| Platinum | Partner logo only | WHSPR Assist attribution in fine print only (e.g., footer, back panel, About screen) |

### 2.4 Prohibited Logo Treatments

The following treatments are not permitted under any circumstances:

- Altering the WHSPR Assist logo proportions, colors, or typeface
- Placing the WHSPR Assist logo on patterned or photographic backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Rotating, skewing, or adding effects (shadows, gradients, outlines) to the WHSPR Assist logo
- Using outdated versions of the WHSPR Assist logo (current version available in the partner portal)
- Combining the WHSPR Assist logo with other third-party brand logos without prior written approval
- Using the WHSPR Assist logo as a decorative element or repeating pattern

## 3. Naming Conventions

Consistent naming across partner materials ensures clarity for patients and protects both the partner and WHSPR Assist brands.

### 3.1 Product Naming by Tier

| Tier | Product Name in Materials | Example |
|------|--------------------------|---------|
| Silver | "WHSPR Assist, available at [Partner Name]" | "WHSPR Assist, available at Clearview Audiology" |
| Gold | "[Partner Name] Hearing Glasses, featuring WHSPR Assist technology" | "Clearview Hearing Glasses, featuring WHSPR Assist technology" |
| Platinum | "[Partner Name] Hearing Glasses" (WHSPR Assist in fine print only) | "Clearview Hearing Glasses" (footer: "Hearing technology by WHSPR Assist") |

### 3.2 Naming Rules

- The term "Hearing Glasses" is the recommended product category name. Partners may substitute "Smart Glasses," "Hearing Eyewear," or "Vision and Hearing Glasses" with prior approval.
- Partners must not use "WHSPR" or "WHSPR Assist" as part of their own brand name, product line name, or domain name.
- The WHSPR Assist name must always appear with the correct capitalization: "WHSPR Assist" (all caps for WHSPR, title case for Assist). Never "Whspr," "whspr assist," or "WHSPR ASSIST."
- When referencing the technology platform, use "WHSPR Assist technology" or "WHSPR Assist hearing enhancement." Do not abbreviate to "WHSPR tech" or "Assist technology."

### 3.3 Domain and URL Guidelines

- Partners must not register domains containing "whspr" or "whspr-assist"
- Product landing pages should live on the partner's existing domain (e.g., clearviewaudiology.com/hearing-glasses)
- Affiliate or tracking links provided by WHSPR Assist may be used in digital campaigns

## 4. Photography Standards

All photography used in partner marketing materials depicting WHSPR Assist products or their use must meet the following standards. These standards protect brand integrity and ensure dignified, authentic representation of the target patient demographic.

### 4.1 Model Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|--------------|
| Minimum age | 50 years old (for all models depicted wearing or interacting with the product) |
| Diversity | Materials should represent diverse ethnicities, genders, and body types |
| Setting | Natural environments: home, garden, clinic, cafe, park, community center |
| Activity | Models should be shown in active, engaged scenarios: reading, conversing, walking, dining, using technology |
| Expression | Natural, genuine expressions; smiling is encouraged but not mandatory |

### 4.2 Photography Style

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Lighting | Warm, natural lighting preferred; avoid harsh clinical fluorescent lighting |
| Color temperature | 4000K -- 5500K (warm daylight); consistent with WHSPR Assist Warm Linen (#F5F0E8) palette |
| Background | Clean, uncluttered backgrounds; soft depth of field to emphasize the subject |
| Product visibility | The smart glasses should be clearly visible but not the sole focus; show the person, not just the product |
| Post-processing | Minimal retouching; maintain natural skin texture and age-appropriate appearance |

### 4.3 Prohibited Imagery

The following types of imagery are not permitted in any partner materials:

- Patronizing poses: exaggerated "thumbs up," surprised expressions at technology, childlike wonder at basic features
- Clinical sterility: patients in hospital gowns, sterile white rooms, medical equipment prominently displayed
- Isolation: patients depicted alone in empty spaces; always show social context or purposeful activity
- Before/after framing: do not imply the product "cures" hearing loss or vision problems
- Stock photography cliches: couples walking on a beach at sunset, seniors at a computer looking confused
- Any imagery depicting users under age 50 wearing the product in marketing materials (product lifestyle shots without visible users are exempt)

### 4.4 Photography Resources

WHSPR Assist provides a licensed photography library to all partners via the partner portal. This library includes:

- 50+ lifestyle images featuring diverse models aged 50 -- 80+
- Product-only shots (all frame models, all colorways, multiple angles)
- Environmental shots (clinic setting, home setting, outdoor setting)
- All images are licensed for partner use in marketing materials for the duration of the partner agreement

Partners may commission their own photography provided it meets the standards above and is submitted for approval before use.

## 5. Color Usage

### 5.1 WHSPR Assist Brand Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Trust Navy | #1B365D | Primary brand color; headings, primary buttons, key UI elements |
| Warm Linen | #F5F0E8 | Background color; packaging base; creates warmth and approachability |
| Clarity Teal | #2A9D8F | Accent color; CTAs, highlights, success states |
| Deep Charcoal | #2D2D2D | Body text; secondary elements |
| Soft White | #FAFAFA | Light backgrounds; card surfaces |

### 5.2 Color Application Rules

- **Co-branded materials (Silver, Gold):** Partner colors may be used for partner branding elements (logo, partner name, contact information). WHSPR Assist palette must be used for product imagery, product descriptions, and any WHSPR Assist branded elements.
- **Fully branded materials (Platinum):** Partners may use their own color palette throughout. WHSPR Assist fine print attribution should use Deep Charcoal (#2D2D2D) or the partner's body text color.
- **Product UI elements:** In app screenshots or UI representations used in marketing, the actual app color scheme must be shown (standard WHSPR Assist for Silver, branded accent for Gold, fully skinned for Platinum). Do not mock up UI colors that differ from the actual patient experience.

### 5.3 Accessibility

All text and interactive elements in partner materials must meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast standards:

- Normal text (under 18pt): minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio against background
- Large text (18pt+ or 14pt bold): minimum 3:1 contrast ratio
- Interactive elements: minimum 3:1 contrast ratio against adjacent colors

## 6. Regulatory and Claims Compliance

### 6.1 Permitted Claims

Partners may make the following claims in marketing materials:

- "Hearing enhancement" or "hearing assistance"
- "Vision assistance" or "vision support"
- "Hearing profile personalization"
- "Prescription-compatible smart glasses"
- "Lightweight assistive eyewear"
- "OTC hearing enhancement" (where applicable per local regulation)

### 6.2 Prohibited Claims

The following claims are prohibited in all partner materials:

| Prohibited Claim | Reason |
|-----------------|--------|
| "Medical device" or "FDA-approved" | WHSPR Assist is not classified as a medical device unless the partner has obtained independent classification |
| "Cures hearing loss" or "restores hearing" | Product enhances hearing; it does not treat, cure, or restore |
| "Replaces hearing aids" | Product complements or serves as an alternative for mild-to-moderate hearing enhancement; not a replacement for prescribed hearing aids |
| "Clinically proven" | Unless referencing a specific published study with partner approval |
| "Doctor recommended" | Unless the specific recommending doctor is identified and has provided written consent |
| Reference to competitor products by name | Do not name competing hearing aid or smart glasses brands in marketing materials |

### 6.3 Disclaimers

All marketing materials must include the following disclaimer in legible type (minimum 8pt print, 11px screen):

> "WHSPR Assist is a hearing enhancement and vision assistance wearable. It is not a medical device and is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any medical condition. Consult a licensed hearing care professional for hearing health concerns."

Platinum partners using their own brand name must append: "Hearing technology by WHSPR Assist."

## 7. Material Approval Process

### 7.1 Submission Requirements

| Material Type | Approval Required | Turnaround |
|--------------|------------------|------------|
| Digital ads (social, display, search) | Yes | 3 business days |
| Print materials (brochures, flyers) | Yes | 3 business days |
| Website content (landing pages, product pages) | Yes | 3 business days |
| Video content (product demos, testimonials) | Yes | 5 business days |
| Press releases or media statements | Yes | 5 business days |
| In-clinic signage | Yes (first instance); subsequent updates exempt if using approved templates | 3 business days |
| Email campaigns | Yes (first instance per template); subsequent sends exempt | 3 business days |

### 7.2 Approval Workflow

1. Partner submits materials through the partner portal with target publication date
2. WHSPR Assist brand team reviews for guideline compliance
3. If approved: partner receives written approval and may proceed with publication
4. If revisions needed: specific feedback provided with revision guidance
5. Revised materials resubmitted for final approval (1 business day turnaround for revisions)

### 7.3 Pre-Approved Templates

WHSPR Assist provides pre-approved templates in the partner portal for common use cases:

- Social media post templates (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn)
- Email announcement templates (patient outreach, clinic newsletter)
- Print ad templates (quarter-page and half-page)
- In-clinic poster templates (A3 and A4)
- Digital display templates (1080x1920 vertical, 1920x1080 horizontal)

Materials created using pre-approved templates without modification do not require individual approval.
