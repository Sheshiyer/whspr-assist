# Features Deep-Dive

> *Hear more. See more. Live more.*

WHSPR Assist Rx packs 13 distinct capabilities into a single frame. Each feature was chosen to address a specific barrier that keeps 80% of hearing-impaired Americans from seeking help.

![Feature Icons — Hearing](/whspr-assist/images/5D-1-hearing-features-icons-flux2pro-v1.png)

---

## Feature-Benefit-Emotion Map

### 1. Dual-Mic ANC/ENC Directional Audio

**What it does:** Two directional microphones with active noise cancellation (ANC) and echo cancellation (ENC) separate the voice in front of you from background noise.

**What it means for you:** Hear the person talking to you in a noisy restaurant. Background clatter fades. Conversations become clear again.

**How it feels:** Relief — the knot in your stomach before dinner out starts to loosen.

**Competitive context:** Same technology used in professional conference headsets. Delivers focused hearing enhancement, not generic amplification like OTC devices.

---

### 2. Prescription-Ready TR Frames

**What it does:** TR thermoplastic frames accept standard optical lab prescription lens grinding — progressive, bifocal, single-vision, reading, and transition lenses.

**What it means for you:** Your optometrist fits these with your lenses. One pair of glasses for everything — distance, reading, hearing, AI.

**How it feels:** Confidence — you walk out with one pair that does it all.

**Competitive context:** No other smart glasses on the market (including Ray-Ban Meta) support prescription lenses for the 50+ demographic.

---

### 3. 1200p Dual Camera System (8MP + 32MP AI)

**What it does:** Dual cameras with AI processing for text recognition (OCR), scene description, object identification, and color recognition.

**What it means for you:** Tap the frame. The AI reads the menu aloud, describes a scene, identifies a street sign. Your glasses whisper what your eyes miss.

**How it feels:** Independence — you did not have to ask anyone for help.

**Competitive context:** Comparable camera-based vision assistants (OrCam $4,500, Envision $3,500) cost 10–13x more and offer no hearing enhancement.

![Feature Icons — Vision](/whspr-assist/images/5D-2-vision-features-icons-flux2pro-v1.png)

---

### 4. Bluetooth 5.4 Connectivity

**What it does:** Latest Bluetooth standard for audio streaming, hands-free calls, and device connectivity.

**What it means for you:** Take calls hands-free. Stream a podcast. Listen to music. No separate earbuds to charge, pair, or lose.

**How it feels:** Convenience — one less thing to manage, carry, or forget.

**Competitive context:** BT 5.4 exceeds Phonak's 5.3 — the newest standard among the world's leading hearing aid manufacturer.

---

### 5. Zero-Stigma Smart Glasses Form Factor

**What it does:** Hearing enhancement technology concealed within a standard eyewear frame. No visible microphones, no ear-canal components, no medical device appearance.

**What it means for you:** These look like regular glasses because they are regular glasses. Nobody knows they do anything more.

**How it feels:** Dignity — the master emotion. You look like you.

**Competitive context:** 66% of people who need hearing help say they would wear a device if nobody could tell. 48% report being mocked or made uncomfortable about hearing difficulties.

---

### 6. Magnetic 2-Pin Charging

**What it does:** Magnetic snap charging system — set glasses on charging stand, pick up when ready. ~60 minutes for full charge.

**What it means for you:** Set your glasses on the stand at night. Pick them up in the morning. No tiny batteries to fumble.

**How it feels:** Ease — one more thing that just works without thinking about it.

**Competitive context:** Eliminates the #4 complaint about traditional hearing aids: battery management.

---

### 7. AI Scene and Text Recognition

**What it does:** Camera-based AI reads text aloud, describes scenes, identifies colors and objects on voice command.

**What it means for you:** Glasses whisper what your eyes miss — menus, street signs, pill bottles, name tags.

**How it feels:** Wonder — your glasses just told you something your eyes could not.

**Use cases:**
- Reading prescription bottle labels at 7 AM
- Getting a menu read aloud in a dimly lit restaurant
- Identifying products on a crowded shelf
- Reading street signs while walking

---

### 8. Four Frame Styles

**What it does:** Classic (square full-rim), Heritage (round full-rim), Refined (semi-rimless), and Active (sport wrap-around).

**What it means for you:** Choose the frame that matches your personality and lifestyle.

**How it feels:** Self-expression — these are your glasses, not a medical appliance.

---

### 9. Audiogram-Based Hearing Profile Calibration

**What it does:** Hearing enhancement tuned to your specific audiogram via the companion app or Provider Portal.

**What it means for you:** Your audiologist tracks your hearing profile over time. Adjustments happen through the app. No extra office visits unless you want them.

**How it feels:** Trust — your healthcare professional is still part of this.

![Feature Icons — Healthcare](/whspr-assist/images/5D-3-healthcare-integration-icons-flux2pro-v1.png)

---

### 10. Provider Portal

**What it does:** Web-based clinical interface for audiogram upload, remote recalibration, patient hearing trend tracking, and engagement analytics.

**What it means for you:** Your clinic can manage your hearing profile remotely, track trends across visits, and make adjustments without requiring an appointment.

---

### 11. Whitelabel Capability

**What it does:** Clinics can brand WHSPR Assist under their own practice name — custom packaging, app skinning, and wake word.

**What it means for you:** The product carries your audiologist's reputation. Recommended by the name you already trust.

---

### 12. Open-Ear Speaker Design

**What it does:** Speakers deliver audio without blocking the ear canal, allowing natural ambient sound plus enhanced audio simultaneously.

**What it means for you:** You hear the world around you and the enhanced conversation at the same time. No occlusion effect.

---

### 13. Hey Cyan Voice Activation

**What it does:** Voice-activated AI assistant triggered by the wake word "Hey Cyan" (customizable in Platinum whitelabel tier).

**What it means for you:** Hands-free access to text reading, scene description, and AI features.

## Documented Limitations

WHSPR Assist is transparent about what the product does and does not do:

- **Not a hearing aid replacement** — designed for mild-to-moderate hearing difficulty
- **4–5 hour battery life** — covers key social situations (dinner, church, golf, calls), not all-day continuous use
- **No clinical speech-in-noise validation data** — hearing enhancement, not clinical amplification
- **No insurance/Medicare reimbursement pathway** — but at $249–$349, costs less than most hearing aid copays
- **HSA/FSA eligible** — assistive technology qualification
