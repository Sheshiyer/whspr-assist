## The Problem: George's Story

George Mitchell turned sixty-eight last March. His daughter Sarah organized dinner at his favorite Italian place. Fourteen people came. His grandkids. His old golf buddy Ray.

It should have been a good night.

George sat at the center of the table. The restaurant was loud — plates clanking, music from overhead speakers, voices bouncing off tile floors. His granddaughter Lily was telling a story about her school play. She was sitting right next to him.

George heard noise. He heard the shape of words. He caught "play" and "Wednesday" and "costume." He nodded. He smiled at the right moments. He laughed when other people laughed.

He did not hear the story.

Halfway through dinner, Lily turned to him and said, "So will you come, Grandpa?" George looked at Sarah. Sarah mouthed the words slowly. "Of course I'll be there, sweetheart," George said.

He spent the rest of the dinner cutting his chicken and smiling. On the drive home, his wife Margaret said, "You were quiet tonight." George said he was just tired.

This is what it looks like when someone starts pulling away from the people they love most. Not because they want to. Because the world got louder and their ears did not keep up.

## The Solution: How WHSPR Assist Works

WHSPR Assist Rx looks like a pair of well-made prescription glasses. Because that is what they are.

Two directional microphones use active noise cancellation to separate the voice in front of you from everything else. Your audiologist calibrates the hearing profile to your specific audiogram. This is not one-size-fits-all amplification. This is frequency shaping matched to your hearing.

When the menu print is too small or a prescription label needs reading, you tap the frame. The AI reads the text aloud through the open-ear speakers. Quietly. Just for you.

**What about the battery?**

**Does insurance cover this?**
At $249–$349, it costs less than most hearing aid copays. HSA/FSA eligible. No paperwork.

**Can I use my prescription?**
Yes. Any optical lab can fit your prescription lenses — progressive, bifocal, single-vision, reading, or transitions.

Meta offers no prescription lenses, no dedicated hearing enhancement, no clinical distribution, and does not target the 50+ hearing demographic.

Cameras activate only on command. No continuous recording. Visible LED indicator. Same model as Ray-Ban Meta.
