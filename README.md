## About

**WHSPR Assist** is a prescription-ready smart glasses brand built for adults 55–75 experiencing mild-to-moderate hearing loss and age-related vision changes. We combine clinical-grade hearing enhancement, AI-powered vision assistance, and everyday eyewear in a single frame — for under $350.

Where traditional hearing aids cost $3,200+ and carry lasting social stigma, WHSPR Assist delivers the same clarity through glasses that look and feel like the ones you already wear. No one knows. Everyone notices the difference.

### Why It Matters

- **466 million** people worldwide experience hearing loss
- **80%** of those who need hearing devices don't use them — cost and stigma are the top barriers
- **71%** of adults over 60 already wear prescription glasses
- The average person waits **7–10 years** after diagnosis before getting a hearing aid

WHSPR Assist closes these gaps by meeting people where they already are: at their optometrist's office, wearing their everyday glasses.

## Brand Assets

### Phase 1 — Market Research & Foundation

Research and validation artifacts that inform every downstream decision:

- **Buyer Persona** — George Mitchell, 68, retired engineer. Represents the core audience of adults 55–75 navigating hearing loss with dignity.
- **Competitor Analysis** — Landscape of hearing aids (Phonak, ReSound), OTC devices, and smart glasses (Ray-Ban Meta, Xreal).
- **Product Description** — Feature-by-feature specification with benefits mapping.
- **Niche Validation** — Reddit/forum complaint cluster analysis and viability scoring.

### Phase 2 — Brand Strategy

Positioning, messaging, and voice assets:

- **Product Positioning** — CBBE framework analysis (Salience → Resonance).
- **Messaging Direction Summary** — Pitch, features, use cases, objections, USP, and emotional drivers.
- **Voice & Tone** — Dr. Maren: a 52-year-old optometrist who speaks like a trusted clinician talking to someone she's known for years. Direct, warm, free of jargon, never condescending.

### Phase 3 — Visual Identity

The complete visual language of WHSPR Assist:

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|

| **Secondary** | Warm Linen `#F5F0E8` — approachable warmth |
| **Accent** | Clarity Teal `#2A9D8F` — CTAs, hearing highlights |
| **Headers** | Source Serif 4 (SemiBold) |
| **Body** | Source Sans 3 (Regular/SemiBold) |
| **Theme** | *Gentle Clarity* — warm clinical aesthetic without sterile coldness |

### Phase 4 — Campaign

### Phase 5 — Email

## B2B Partner Program

WHSPR Assist is delivered through a **healthcare professional channel** — audiologists, optometrists, and hearing clinics prescribe and fit the product directly. The `06-b2b-whitelabel/` directory contains the complete partner program:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Partner Program Overview** | Revenue model, tiers, onboarding, and support |
| **API Integration Spec** | Provider Portal integration for clinic systems |
| **Clinic Deployment Guide** | Physical setup, display, and patient flow |
| **Co-Branding Guidelines** | How partners apply their branding within WHSPR guidelines |
| **Partner Marketing Kit** | Ready-to-use marketing materials for clinics |
| **Product App Skinning** | White-label companion app customization |

Partners benefit from:
- **Professional fitting** reduces return rates from 35–50% (OTC) to 6–10%
- **Provider Portal** enables patient engagement tracking and upsell pipeline
- **Prescription-ready frames** work with any optical lab — no proprietary lens system
- **Clinic-level switching costs** no competitor can match

## Brand Configuration

The `brand-config.yaml` file is the single source of truth for the WHSPR Assist brand system. It drives all downstream content and visual asset generation, containing:

- **Execution context** — Budget tier, launch channel, maturity stage, quality bar
- **Brand identity** — Name, tagline, archetype, voice persona, tone calibration
- **Visual theme** — Color palette, typography, photography and illustration style
- **Products** — Hero product and capsule lineup specifications
- **Audience** — Persona details, aspirations, pain points, aesthetic affinities
- **Competitive context** — Differentiation pillars and territory claims
- **Generation config** — Visual asset prompts, seeds, resolution, and output settings
